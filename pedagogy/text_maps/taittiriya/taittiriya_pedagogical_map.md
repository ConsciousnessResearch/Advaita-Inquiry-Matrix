
# AIM Pedagogical Map — Taittirīya Upaniṣad

## Overview

The *Taittirīya Upaniṣad* encodes a complete Advaita teaching cycle.  
Its three vallīs form a structured progression from preparation to realization and integration.

```
Preparation → Inquiry → Experiential Verification → Realization → Integration
```

---

## 1. Śikṣāvallī — Preparation of the Student

**Pedagogical Mode:** Ethical and contemplative preparation  
**Purpose:** Establish the adhikāra (qualification) of the student.

### Key Functions
- Guru–śiṣya relationship
- Discipline of speech and recitation
- Ethical foundation
- Symbolic contemplation

### AIM Clusters Activated
- `guru_transmission`
- `ethical_foundation`
- `symbolic_contemplation`
- `cosmic_contemplation`

---

## 2. Ānandavallī — Philosophical Inquiry

**Pedagogical Mode:** Analytical discrimination and metaphysical definition.

### Core Inquiry

```
yato vā imāni bhūtāni jāyante
yena jātāni jīvanti
yat prayanty abhisaṃviśanti
```

### Pañca‑Kośa Analysis

| Layer | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| annamaya | physical body |
| prāṇamaya | life force |
| manomaya | mind |
| vijñānamaya | intellect |
| ānandamaya | bliss |

### Culmination
- Brahman identified with **ānanda**
- Bliss hierarchy (*ānanda‑mīmāṃsā*)
- Transcendence of mind and speech

### AIM Clusters Activated
- `identity_analysis`
- `brahman_definition`
- `experiential_scale`
- `realization_statement`

## 3. Bhṛguvallī — Experiential Verification

**Pedagogical Mode:** Contemplative experiment.

Varuṇa instructs Bhṛgu:

```
tapasā brahma vijijñāsasva
```

### Inquiry Cycle

| Stage | Discovery |
|------|-----------|
| 1 | annam brahma |
| 2 | prāṇa brahma |
| 3 | manas brahma |
| 4 | vijñāna brahma |
| 5 | ānanda brahma |

### Integration After Realization

The Upaniṣad turns toward life practice:

| Teaching |
|----------|
| Do not disparage food |
| Do not reject food |
| Produce abundant food |
| Feed the guest |

### Final Expression

```
aham annam
aham annādo
```
