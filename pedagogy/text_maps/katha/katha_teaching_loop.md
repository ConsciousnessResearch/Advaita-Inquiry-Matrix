# Kaṭha Upaniṣad --- Teaching Loop

## Purpose

This document describes the **core pedagogical loop** used in the
dialogue between **Yama and Naciketas** in the Kaṭha Upaniṣad.

Unlike Chāndogya Upaniṣad 6, which uses a repeated **analogy →
mahāvākya** cycle, Kaṭha employs a **discrimination-based teaching
loop**. The teacher progressively removes layers of misunderstanding
through *viveka* (discriminative insight) until the nature of the
**Ātman** becomes clear.

This teaching structure makes Kaṭha an important **second pedagogical
engine** for the AIM system.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Core Discrimination Loop

The instructional pattern can be summarized as:

student doubt\
↓\
discrimination principle (viveka)\
↓\
description of the Self\
↓\
removal of mistaken identification

This cycle repeats across the dialogue, each time addressing a deeper
misconception.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Pedagogical Structure of the Dialogue

The Kaṭha Upaniṣad unfolds in several teaching cycles.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Cycle 1 --- Ethical Discrimination

**Verses:** 1.2.1--1.2.3

Teaching principle:

śreyaḥ vs preyaḥ\
(the good vs the merely pleasant)

Purpose:

Distinguish between what is **truly beneficial** and what merely
satisfies desire.

Typical AIM tags:

``` yaml
cluster: ethical_discrimination
prakriyā: śreya_preya_viveka
pedagogical_role: student_qualification
```

Obstacle removed:

confusion between **spiritual good and worldly pleasure**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Cycle 2 --- Ignorance vs Knowledge

**Verses:** 1.2.4--1.2.6

Teaching principle:

avidyā vs vidyā

Purpose:

Show that most people believe themselves knowledgeable while remaining
in ignorance.

Obstacle removed:

false confidence in conventional knowledge.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Cycle 3 --- Immortality of the Self

**Verses:** 1.2.18--1.2.25

Key teaching:

*na jāyate mriyate vā kadācit*

"The Self is never born and never dies."

Purpose:

Remove fear of death by revealing the unborn nature of the Self.

Typical AIM tags:

``` yaml
cluster: self_description
prakriyā: immortality_teaching
```

Obstacle removed:

identification with the mortal body.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Cycle 4 --- The Chariot Analogy

**Verses:** 1.3.3--1.3.9

Teaching device:

chariot metaphor

Structure:

body → chariot\
senses → horses\
mind → reins\
intellect → driver\
Self → master of the chariot

Typical AIM tag:

``` yaml
teaching_device: analogy
prakriyā: hierarchy_discrimination
```

Purpose:

Clarify the **internal hierarchy of the person**.

Obstacle removed:

confusion between Self, mind, and senses.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Cycle 5 --- Subtlety of the Self

**Verses:** 1.3.10--1.3.15

Teaching:

The Self is subtler than intellect and cannot be grasped as an object.

Purpose:

Prevent the student from objectifying the Self.

Obstacle removed:

attempt to perceive the Self as an external object.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Cycle 6 --- Final Instruction

**Verses:** 2.3.8--2.3.14

Famous exhortation:

*uttiṣṭhata jāgrata prāpya varān nibodhata*

"Arise! Awake! Approach the wise and understand."

Purpose:

Encourage direct realization.

Obstacle removed:

spiritual complacency.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Algorithmic Representation

The Kaṭha teaching process can be expressed as:

    test student qualification

    while identification_with_nonself:

        introduce discrimination principle
        describe the nature of the Self
        remove mistaken identification

The dialogue ends when the student's understanding stabilizes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Comparison with Chāndogya Teaching Loop

  Feature              Chāndogya 6          Kaṭha
  -------------------- -------------------- ------------------
  Primary method       analogy repetition   discrimination
  Key phrase           tat tvam asi         śreyaḥ vs preyaḥ
  Pedagogical engine   assimilation loop    viveka loop
  Teacher              Uddālaka             Yama
  Student              Śvetaketu            Naciketas

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Significance for AIM

The Kaṭha Upaniṣad provides a **discrimination-based teaching engine**.

This engine is particularly useful when a student:

-   struggles with existential questions
-   fears death
-   confuses Self with body or mind

In such cases, the system can apply **viveka-oriented teaching
sequences** derived from Kaṭha rather than analogy-based instruction.

Together with the Chāndogya teaching loop, this forms the foundation for
a **multi-strategy Advaita pedagogy system** inside AIM.
