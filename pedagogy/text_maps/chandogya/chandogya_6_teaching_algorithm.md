# Chāndogya Upaniṣad 6 --- Teaching Algorithm

## Purpose

This document describes how **Chāndogya Upaniṣad Chapter 6** implements
the universal Upaniṣadic teaching template.\
The dialogue between **Uddālaka Āruṇi and Śvetaketu** provides one of
the clearest models of Advaita pedagogy.

The chapter can be understood as a **recursive teaching loop** designed
to remove layers of misunderstanding until the identity of **Ātman and
Brahman** becomes evident.

# Phase 1 --- Diagnostic Exposure

**Verses:** 6.1.1--6.1.3

Uddālaka observes that Śvetaketu has returned from study proud of his
learning.

He asks:

"Did you ask for that knowledge by which the unheard becomes heard?"

Purpose:

Expose the limitation of **mere intellectual knowledge**.

AIM tags:

    pedagogical_role: diagnostic_entry
    pedagogical_stage: ignorance_exposure

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Phase 2 --- Ontological Principle

**Verses:** 6.1.4--6.2

The teacher introduces the principle of **cause--effect
non‑difference**.

Examples:

-   clay → pots
-   gold → ornaments
-   iron → tools

Key statement:

*mṛttiketyeva satyam*\
"Clay alone is real."

Purpose:

Introduce **kārya--kāraṇa-ananyatva**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Phase 3 --- Cosmological Projection

**Verses:** 6.3--6.4

Explanation of the emergence of the universe from **sat**.

Sequence:

sat → tejas → ap → anna

Purpose:

Resolve the student's question about **multiplicity**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Phase 4 --- Microcosm Explanation

**Verses:** 6.5--6.6

The cosmic elements correspond to functions within the body.

Examples:

food → mind\
water → prāṇa\
fire → speech

Purpose:

Bridge **cosmology and human experience**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Phase 5 --- Experiential Demonstration

**Verses:** 6.7

Śvetaketu undergoes a fifteen‑day fast.

Result:

His inability to recall the Veda demonstrates that **mind depends on
food**.

Purpose:

Move from theory to **direct observation**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Phase 6 --- Identity Teaching Loop

**Verses:** 6.8--6.16

The teacher now begins a repeated instructional cycle.

Algorithm:

    introduce analogy
    ↓
    state mahāvākya
    ↓
    student asks for clarification
    ↓
    repeat with new analogy

Mahāvākya:

*tat tvam asi śvetaketo*

This cycle occurs **nine times**.

# Phase 7 --- Teacher Necessity

**Verse:** 6.14

The story of the blindfolded traveler illustrates that guidance from a
teacher is necessary to reach the destination.

# Phase 8 --- Dissolution Process

**Verse:** 6.15

Explanation of the process of death:

speech → mind → prāṇa → fire → sat

Purpose:

Remove fear of death and reinforce the identity with **sat**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Phase 9 --- Truth Alignment

**Verse:** 6.16

The hot‑axe ordeal demonstrates that **truth protects the knower of
reality**.

Purpose:

Align realization with **satya (truthfulness)**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Realization Confirmation

Final statement:

*taddhāsya vijajñāva*

"Śvetaketu understood."

This indicates successful completion of the teaching process.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Algorithmic Summary

The pedagogical process of the chapter can be expressed as:

    diagnose ignorance
    introduce ontological principle
    explain cosmology
    map macrocosm to microcosm
    demonstrate principle experientially

    while student requests clarification:
        present analogy
        reveal identity (tat tvam asi)

    confirm role of teacher
    explain death process
    affirm truth alignment
    confirm realization

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Significance for AIM

Chāndogya 6 provides a **reference implementation** of the Upaniṣadic
teaching method.

It demonstrates how a teacher:

-   diagnoses misunderstanding
-   uses analogies strategically
-   repeats identity statements until assimilation occurs
-   stabilizes realization through ethical alignment.

For AIM, this chapter serves as a **pedagogical engine** that can guide
dialogue with students.
