# Chāndogya Upaniṣad 6 --- Teaching Loop

## Purpose

This document describes the **recursive teaching loop** used by Uddālaka
Āruṇi in Chāndogya Upaniṣad Chapter 6 when instructing Śvetaketu.

After establishing the metaphysical foundation, the teacher repeatedly
applies a **cycle of analogy and identity revelation** to dissolve the
student's remaining misconceptions.

This loop forms the **core pedagogical engine** of the chapter.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Core Teaching Loop

The instructional pattern used in the chapter can be expressed as:

analogy\
↓\
identity statement (*mahāvākya*)\
↓\
student requests clarification\
↓\
new analogy

This cycle repeats until the student assimilates the teaching.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Loop Structure

## Step 1 --- Introduce Analogy

The teacher introduces a concrete illustration (*dṛṣṭānta*) drawn from
everyday experience.

Purpose:

-   make subtle metaphysics intelligible
-   bypass abstract resistance
-   reveal hidden identity

Example analogies include:

-   honey produced by many bees
-   rivers entering the ocean
-   banyan seed containing an invisible essence
-   salt dissolved in water

Typical AIM tag:

``` yaml
teaching_device: analogy
```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Step 2 --- Reveal Identity

After the analogy clarifies the principle, the teacher states the
identity directly.

The repeated mahāvākya:

*tat tvam asi śvetaketo*\
"Thou art That, Śvetaketu."

Typical AIM tag:

``` yaml
prakriyā: mahāvākya_revelation
pedagogical_role: identity_revelation
```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Step 3 --- Student Requests Clarification

Śvetaketu repeatedly asks for further explanation:

"Please explain this again, revered sir."

This indicates that the teaching has not yet been fully assimilated.

Pedagogical significance:

-   recognition that realization requires repetition
-   acknowledgment of remaining doubt

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Step 4 --- Repeat with New Analogy

The teacher introduces another analogy targeting a different
misunderstanding.

Each analogy dissolves a different cognitive obstacle.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Pedagogical Logic

The loop functions because each analogy removes a **specific
misconception**.

  Analogy             Misconception Removed
  ------------------- --------------------------------
  deep sleep          Self confused with waking mind
  honey               individuality illusion
  rivers              apparent separation
  tree                life vs body confusion
  banyan seed         invisibility of cause
  salt water          unseen presence of reality
  Gandhāra            necessity of guidance
  death dissolution   fear of death
  hot axe             truth alignment

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Algorithmic Representation

The teaching loop can be expressed programmatically as:

    while student_has_doubt:

        present analogy
        reveal identity (tat tvam asi)
        student asks for clarification

The loop ends when:

    student assimilates identity

The Upaniṣad concludes with:

*taddhāsya vijajñāva*\
"Śvetaketu understood."

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Significance for AIM

This teaching loop is a powerful model for an Advaita dialogue system.

Rather than repeating doctrine, the system can:

1.  diagnose the student's confusion
2.  select an appropriate analogy
3.  restate the identity teaching
4.  continue until understanding stabilizes

Thus the system replicates the **traditional guru--śiṣya pedagogical
rhythm**.
