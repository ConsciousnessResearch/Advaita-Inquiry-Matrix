# Prakriyā Taxonomy for Advaita Inquiry Matrix (AIM)

This document defines the **taxonomy of teaching methods (prakriyā-s)**
used in Advaita Vedānta pedagogy. These methods are the operational
tools used by the teacher to remove ignorance (*avidyā*) and reveal the
identity of ātman and brahman.

The taxonomy is derived from analysis of the following texts currently
ingested into the AIM corpus:

-   Kaṭha Upaniṣad
-   Chāndogya Upaniṣad (Chapter 6)
-   Taittirīya Upaniṣad
-   Aitareya Upaniṣad
-   Māṇḍūkya Upaniṣad
-   Muṇḍaka Upaniṣad
-   Ātma-bodha
-   Upadeśa-sāhasrī

Additional prakriyā-s will appear as further texts (e.g. Bṛhadāraṇyaka)
are ingested.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Core Prakriyā Set

## 1. dṛg--dṛśya viveka

**Principle**\
The seer (*dṛk*) is never the seen (*dṛśya*).

**Function**\
Separates the witnessing consciousness from the body--mind complex.

**Example texts**\
Kaṭha 1.3\
Ātma-bodha\
Upadeśa-sāhasrī

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. avasthā-traya prakriyā

**Principle**\
Analysis of the three states of experience:

-   jāgrat (waking)
-   svapna (dream)
-   suṣupti (deep sleep)

**Function**\
Reveals consciousness as constant across changing experiential states.

**Example text**\
Māṇḍūkya Upaniṣad

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. pañca-kośa prakriyā

**Principle**\
Identity is progressively negated through five sheaths:

-   annamaya
-   prāṇamaya
-   manomaya
-   vijñānamaya
-   ānandamaya

**Function**\
Removes layered misidentifications with bodily and mental structures.

**Example text**\
Taittirīya Upaniṣad (Ānandavallī)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. kāraṇa--kārya prakriyā

**Principle**\
The effect is non-separate from its cause.

Classic analogies:

-   clay → pot
-   gold → ornaments

**Function**\
Demonstrates that the world is non-separate from brahman.

**Example text**\
Chāndogya Upaniṣad 6

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. mahāvākya prakriyā

Identity statements revealing the nonduality of Self and Absolute.

Canonical mahāvākya-s:

-   *tat tvam asi*
-   *ahaṃ brahmāsmi*
-   *prajñānam brahma*
-   *ayam ātmā brahma*

**Function**\
Directly reveals the identity of ātman and brahman.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. neti--neti prakriyā

**Principle**\
Negation of all objectifiable phenomena.

**Function**\
Prevents brahman from being mistaken for an object of knowledge.

**Example text**\
Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. dṛṣṭānta prakriyā (analogy method)

Teaching through analogies drawn from everyday experience.

Common examples:

-   clay--pot
-   gold--ornament
-   salt dissolved in water
-   chariot metaphor
-   sparks from fire

**Function**\
Makes subtle metaphysical insights accessible through perceptible
models.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. adhyāropa--apavāda prakriyā

**Principle**\
Provisional superimposition followed by later negation.

Teaching structure:

provisional explanation\
→ gradual refinement\
→ final negation

**Function**\
Guides the student step-by-step toward nondual recognition.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Extended Prakriyā Set (Future Corpus Expansion)

These methods will become more prominent once texts like
**Bṛhadāraṇyaka** and **Gauḍapāda Kārikā** are fully integrated.

### anvaya--vyatireka

Co-presence / co-absence reasoning used to isolate the Self from
changing phenomena.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### bhāga-tyāga-lakṣaṇā

Interpretive logic used to resolve apparent contradictions in mahāvākya
statements.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### adhyāsa analysis

Investigation of superimposition (*adhyāsa*) as the fundamental
structure of ignorance.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### sākṣī prakriyā

Recognition of the witnessing consciousness as distinct from mental
activity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### ajātivāda reasoning

Doctrine of non-origination articulated by Gauḍapāda.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Role in the AIM System

Within the Advaita Inquiry Matrix, prakriyā-s function as **pedagogical
operators**.

Operational pipeline:

student input\
→ cognitive error detection\
→ prakriyā selection\
→ scripture routing\
→ dialogical clarification

Thus the prakriyā taxonomy forms a central component of the AIM teaching
engine.
