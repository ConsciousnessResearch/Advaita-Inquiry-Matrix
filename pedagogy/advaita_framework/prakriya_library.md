# Advaita Prakriyā Library

This document catalogs classical **Advaita Vedānta teaching methods
(prakriyā-s)**. Each prakriyā is a pedagogical strategy used to remove
specific misunderstandings (adhyāsa) and guide the student toward
recognition of the Self (ātman = brahman).

The library is intended for use by the AIM Teaching Engine to select
appropriate instructional moves based on the student's cognitive state.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Core Prakriyā Catalogue

## 1. Adhyāropa--Apavāda

**Method:** Superimposition followed by negation.

Teacher first provisionally accepts a conceptual model, then removes it.

Example:

-   Brahman described as creator
-   Later negated as actionless

Purpose:

Remove conceptual dependence while guiding inquiry.

Texts:

-   Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad
-   Gauḍapāda Kārikā

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Dṛg--Dṛśya Viveka

**Method:** Seer--seen discrimination.

Anything perceived cannot be the perceiver.

Progression:

object → senses → mind → witness

Purpose:

Separate Self from body and mind.

Texts:

-   Dṛg‑Dṛśya Viveka
-   Kaṭha Upaniṣad 1.3

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Pañca‑Kośa Analysis

**Method:** Five‑sheath analysis.

Layers:

1.  annamaya
2.  prāṇamaya
3.  manomaya
4.  vijñānamaya
5.  ānandamaya

Purpose:

Disidentify from progressively subtler layers.

Text:

-   Taittirīya Upaniṣad

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Avasthā‑Traya Analysis

**Method:** Analysis of three states.

States:

-   waking
-   dream
-   deep sleep

Purpose:

Reveal the witnessing consciousness present in all states.

Text:

-   Māṇḍūkya Upaniṣad

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Mahāvākya Prakriyā

**Method:** Identity statements reveal nonduality.

Examples:

-   tat tvam asi
-   ahaṁ brahmāsmi
-   ayam ātmā brahma
-   prajñānam brahma

Purpose:

Collapse the apparent difference between jīva and brahman.

Texts:

-   Chāndogya
-   Bṛhadāraṇyaka
-   Māṇḍūkya
-   Aitareya

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Neti Neti

**Method:** Apophatic negation.

Anything objectifiable is rejected as the Self.

Formula:

not this, not this.

Purpose:

Prevent objectification of Brahman.

Text:

-   Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. Kārya‑Kāraṇa Ananyatva

**Method:** Non‑difference of cause and effect.

Example:

Clay and pot.

Purpose:

Show the world is not separate from Brahman.

Texts:

-   Chāndogya Upaniṣad
-   Vedānta commentaries

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Ratha Kalpanā (Chariot Metaphor)

**Method:** Structural metaphor.

Body = chariot\
Senses = horses\
Mind = reins\
Intellect = driver\
Self = lord of the chariot

Purpose:

Clarify instrument vs Self.

Text:

-   Kaṭha Upaniṣad 1.3

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9. Two Birds Analogy

**Method:** Symbolic duality resolving into unity.

One bird eats fruit; the other witnesses.

Purpose:

Illustrate jīva--ātman relationship.

Text:

-   Muṇḍaka Upaniṣad

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 10. Self‑Luminosity Teaching

**Method:** Demonstrate that consciousness reveals all experience.

Key idea:

Consciousness is not illuminated by anything else.

Text:

-   Kaṭha Upaniṣad 2.2.15

------------------------------------------------------------------------
