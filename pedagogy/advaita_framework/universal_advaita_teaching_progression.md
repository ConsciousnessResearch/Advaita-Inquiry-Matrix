# Universal Advaita Teaching Progression

This document describes the **canonical pedagogical progression** that
appears across the Advaita Vedānta corpus.\
The structure emerges from comparative analysis of major texts already
ingested into the AIM corpus, including:

-   Kaṭha Upaniṣad
-   Chāndogya Upaniṣad (Chapter 6)
-   Taittirīya Upaniṣad
-   Aitareya Upaniṣad
-   Muṇḍaka Upaniṣad
-   Ātma‑bodha
-   Upadeśa‑sāhasrī

Although each text uses different narratives and metaphors, they
consistently guide the student through the **same sequence of cognitive
transformations**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Canonical Teaching Sequence

## 1. Existential Destabilization

The teaching begins by undermining the student's confidence in ordinary
life structures.

Typical methods:

-   confrontation with death
-   critique of ritualism
-   dissatisfaction with worldly knowledge

Examples:

-   Kaṭha Upaniṣad 1.1 --- Naciketas confronts death\
-   Muṇḍaka Upaniṣad 1.1 --- distinction between higher and lower
    knowledge\
-   Upadeśa‑sāhasrī --- critique of ritual action

Purpose:

    break the assumption that empirical life provides ultimate fulfillment

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Ethical Discrimination

The student learns to distinguish:

    śreyas  — ultimate good
    preyas  — immediate pleasure

Example:

-   Kaṭha Upaniṣad 1.2

Purpose:

    orient the student's will toward liberation rather than worldly reward

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Instrument Analysis

The teaching separates the Self from the body‑mind complex.

Typical prakriyā:

-   dṛg‑dṛśya viveka
-   structural metaphors

Examples:

-   Kaṭha Upaniṣad 1.3 --- chariot metaphor\
-   Ātma‑bodha --- mind as instrument

Purpose:

    dissolve identification with body and mind

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Ontological Inquiry

Attention shifts from psychology to metaphysics.

Typical methods:

-   cause‑effect analysis
-   cosmological models
-   ontological inquiry

Examples:

-   Aitareya Upaniṣad --- cosmological creation narrative\
-   Chāndogya Upaniṣad 6 --- clay‑pot cause‑effect analysis

Purpose:

    redirect inquiry toward the nature of reality itself

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Progressive Negation

Layers of identity are removed step by step.

Typical prakriyā:

-   pañca‑kośa analysis
-   neti‑neti negation

Example:

-   Taittirīya Upaniṣad --- kośa model

Purpose:

    eliminate increasingly subtle misidentifications

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Witness Recognition

The student recognizes the witnessing consciousness behind experience.

Examples:

-   Māṇḍūkya Upaniṣad --- three states analysis\
-   Kaṭha Upaniṣad 2.1 --- inward recognition

Purpose:

    establish the Self as the knower of all experience

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. Nondual Identity Teaching

The mahāvākya statements reveal the identity of Self and Brahman.

Examples:

-   tat tvam asi --- Chāndogya\
-   ahaṁ brahmāsmi --- Bṛhadāraṇyaka\
-   prajñānam brahma --- Aitareya\
-   ayam ātmā brahma --- Māṇḍūkya

Purpose:

    collapse the apparent difference between individual self and ultimate reality

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Non‑Objectifiability Clarification

The teaching prevents Brahman from being reified as an object.

Examples:

-   Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad --- neti neti\
-   Kaṭha Upaniṣad 2.3 --- Self beyond perception

Purpose:

    prevent the mind from treating Brahman as an experiential object

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9. Stabilization

The final phase integrates the knowledge into lived understanding.

Typical methods:

-   nididhyāsana
-   contemplative assimilation

Examples:

-   Upadeśa‑sāhasrī\
-   Ātma‑bodha

Purpose:

    remove residual confusion and stabilize recognition

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Role in the AIM System

This progression forms the **backbone of the AIM pedagogical model**.

It allows the system to:

1.  Diagnose the student's stage of inquiry
2.  Select the appropriate teaching method (prakriyā)
3.  Route the dialogue to relevant scriptural passages
4.  Conduct guided Advaita inquiry
