
# Cross‑Upaniṣad Pedagogical Index (AIM)

This document maps major Upaniṣads to the pedagogical roles they play in Advaita teaching.
Rather than functioning as isolated texts, the Upaniṣads collectively form a distributed
teaching system guiding the student from preparation to realization and integration.

---

## Stage 1 — Preparation of the Student (Adhikāra Formation)

These texts cultivate ethical stability, reverence for knowledge, and contemplative orientation.

| Upaniṣad | Pedagogical Focus |
|---|---|
| Taittirīya (Śikṣāvallī) | discipline, reverence for knowledge |
| Chāndogya (early sections) | symbolic meditation and cosmology |
| Bṛhadāraṇyaka (selected dialogues) | renunciation and existential inquiry |

Typical AIM clusters:

```
guru_transmission
ethical_foundation
symbolic_contemplation
cosmic_contemplation
```

---

## Stage 2 — Identity Analysis

These Upaniṣads dismantle mistaken identification and refine understanding of the Self.

| Upaniṣad | Method |
|---|---|
| Taittirīya (Ānandavallī) | pañca‑kośa analysis |
| Bṛhadāraṇyaka | neti‑neti discrimination |
| Chāndogya (tat tvam asi passages) | identity teaching |
| Aitareya | creation → consciousness argument |

Typical AIM clusters:

```
identity_analysis
brahman_definition
```

---

## Stage 3 — Radical Nondual Insight

These texts present realization directly and uncompromisingly.

| Upaniṣad | Core Teaching |
|---|---|
| Māṇḍūkya | turīya beyond waking, dream, and deep sleep |
| Aṣṭāvakra Gītā | pure witness consciousness |
| Avadhūta Gītā | uncompromising nondualism |

Typical AIM clusters:

```
direct_realization
nondual_identity
```

---

## Stage 4 — Experiential Inquiry

These texts guide the aspirant through discovery rather than only explaining doctrine.

| Upaniṣad | Method |
|---|---|
| Taittirīya (Bhṛguvallī) | contemplative experiment |
| Kaṭha | dialogue with Yama |
| Praśna | structured inquiry cycles |

Typical AIM clusters:

```
contemplative_inquiry
guided_realization
```

---

## Stage 5 — Integration of Realization

These teachings describe the life and conduct of one established in knowledge.

| Upaniṣad | Focus |
|---|---|
| Taittirīya (Bhṛguvallī end) | food ethics and hospitality |
| Bṛhadāraṇyaka (Yājñavalkya teachings) | freedom beyond duality |
| Īśa | action without attachment |

Typical AIM clusters:

```
ethical_expression_of_realization
liberated_life
```

---

## Summary Teaching Arc

```
Preparation
↓
Identity discrimination
↓
Direct insight
↓
Experiential verification
↓
Integration into life
```

The *Taittirīya Upaniṣad* is especially important because it contains almost the entire
pedagogical arc internally, making it a structural reference text for Advaita pedagogy.
