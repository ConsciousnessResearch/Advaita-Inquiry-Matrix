# Advaita Inquiry Matrix (AIM)

## Brand Specification v1.0

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. Core Identity

**Name:** Advaita Inquiry Matrix
**Acronym:** AIM
**Primary Mark:** aiṁ (ऐँ) glyph in Devanāgarī
**Conceptual Basis:** Discriminative clarity (viveka) in transmission

The aiṁ glyph represents:

-   Refined cognition
-   Alignment with knowledge
-   Commitment to śāstra-pramāṇa
-   Structured inquiry

The symbol is orienting, not mystical.
It does not imply metaphysical power.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Visual Tone

AIM visual identity must communicate:

-   Intellectual seriousness
-   Calm authority
-   Structural precision
-   Non-devotional neutrality
-   Long-term durability

It must avoid:

-   Glows
-   Mystical aura effects
-   Cosmic symbolism
-   Tech-startup excess
-   Decorative overlays

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Primary Logo Version

### Configuration

-   Black aiṁ glyph
-   Warm neutral parchment field
-   Generous negative space
-   No drop shadows
-   No ornament

### Color Reference (Suggested)

Background base:
HEX range approximately #E9DDCC to #F1E6D6

Glyph:
Deep neutral black (#111111 or #000000)

Texture:
Subtle grain ≤ 10% visibility
Must remain invisible at small scale.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Secondary Logo Variants

### A. Academic / Print Version

Black glyph on pure white background.

Use for:

-   Formal documents
-   PDFs
-   Academic publications
-   Book covers

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### B. Dark Mode Version

White glyph on deep charcoal or black background.

Suggested:

Background: #111111
Glyph: #FFFFFF

Use for:

-   App dark mode
-   Splash screens
-   Video intros

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### C. Monochrome Flat Version

Single-color vector version without texture.

Use for:

-   Small icon usage
-   Favicon
-   Embroidery
-   Seal/stamp applications

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Usage Hierarchy

The aiṁ glyph should appear in:

-   Splash screen
-   Opening screen of software
-   Cover page of formal documents
-   Video intro frame

It should not:

-   Be tiled repeatedly
-   Be used as decorative wallpaper
-   Appear inside body content
-   Be excessively animated

The glyph marks entry into the inquiry field.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Spatial Rules

Maintain minimum clear space around the glyph equal to at least the
width of the bindu above the glyph.

Do not crowd the mark.

Negative space is essential to authority.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. Typography Pairing (Optional Guidance)

If pairing with English text:

Avoid:

-   Ornamental Sanskrit-style fonts
-   Decorative serif excess

Prefer:

-   Clean serif (for gravitas)
-   Neutral sans-serif (for system feel)

Text must remain secondary to the glyph.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Tagline Usage (Optional)

Possible restrained taglines:

-   Structured Advaita Inquiry
-   A Pramāṇa Environment
-   Semantic Architecture for Non-Dual Inquiry

Tagline must remain visually subordinate to the glyph.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9. Public Framing Statement

AIM (Advaita Inquiry Matrix) is a structured pramāṇa-environment
designed to support disciplined non-dual inquiry.

The aiṁ glyph reflects the project's commitment to clarity in
transmission.

No metaphysical claims are implied by the symbol.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 10. Identity Discipline Rule

The AIM logo must feel:

-   Stable in 20 years
-   Appropriate for academic contexts
-   Serious enough for śāstra
-   Neutral enough for technology

If future iterations drift toward excess, revert to this specification
(v1.0).
