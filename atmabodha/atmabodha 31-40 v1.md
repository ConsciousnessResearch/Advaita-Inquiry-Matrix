## Verse 31

**Sanskrit**

आविद्यकं शरीरादि दृश्यं बुद्बुदवत्क्षरम्।
एतद्विलक्षणं विद्यादहं ब्रह्मेति निर्मलम् ॥३१॥

**IAST**

āvidyakaṃ śarīrādi dṛśyaṃ budbudavatkṣaram.
etadvilakṣaṇaṃ vidyādahaṃ brahmeti nirmalam || 31 ||

**Translation**

The body, etc., up to the "Causal Body" - Ignorance - which are objects perceived, are as perishable as bubbles. Realise through discrimination that I am the'Pure Brahman' ever completely separate from all these.

## Verse 32

**Sanskrit**

देहान्यत्वान्न मे जन्मजराकार्श्यलयादयः।
शब्दादिविषयैः सङ्गो निरिन्द्रियतया न च ॥३२॥

**IAST**

dehānyatvānna me janmajarākārśyalayādayaḥ.
śabdādiviṣayaiḥ saṅgo nirindriyatayā na ca || 32 ||

**Translation**

I am other than the body and so I am free from changes such as birth, wrinkling, senility, death, etc. I have nothing to do with the sense objects such as sound and taste, for I am without the sense-organs.

## Verse 33

**Sanskrit**

अमनस्त्वान्न मे दुःखरागद्वेषभयादयः।
अप्राणो ह्यमनाः शुभ्र इत्यादिश्रुतिशासनात् ॥३३॥

**IAST**

amanastvānna me duḥkharāgadveṣabhayādayaḥ.
aprāṇo hyamanāḥ śubhra ityādiśrutiśāsanāt || 33 ||

**Translation**

I am other than the mind and hence, I am free from sorrow, attachment, malice and fear, for "HE is without breath and without mind, Pure, etc.", is the Commandment of the great scripture, the Upanishads.

## Verse 34

**Sanskrit**

निर्गुणो निष्क्रियो नित्यो निर्विकल्पो निरञ्जनः।
निर्विकारो निराकारो नित्यमुक्तोऽस्मि निर्मलः ॥३४॥

**IAST**

nirguṇo niṣkriyo nityo nirvikalpo nirañjanaḥ.
nirvikāro nirākāro nityamukto'smi nirmalaḥ || 34 ||

**Translation**

I am without attributes and actions; Eternal (Nitya) without any desire and thought (Nirvikalpa), without any dirt (Niranjana), without any change (Nirvikara), without form (Nirakara), ever-liberated (Nitya Mukta) ever-pure (Nirmala).

## Verse 35

**Sanskrit**

अहमाकाशवत्सर्वं बहिरन्तर्गतोऽच्युतः।
सदा सर्वसमः सिद्धो निःसङ्गो निर्मलोऽचलः ॥३५॥

**IAST**

ahamākāśavatsarvaṃ bahirantargato'cyutaḥ.
sadā sarvasamaḥ siddho niḥsaṅgo nirmalo'calaḥ || 35 ||

**Translation**

Like the space I fill all things within and without. Changeless and the same in all, at all times I am pure, unattached, stainless and motionless.

## Verse 37

**Sanskrit**

एवं निरन्तरकृता ब्रह्मैवास्मीति वासना।
हरत्यविद्याविक्षेपान्रोगानिव रसायनम् ॥३७॥

**IAST**

evaṃ nirantarakṛtā brahmaivāsmīti vāsanā.
haratyavidyāvikṣepānrogāniva rasāyanam || 37 ||

**Translation**

The impression "I am Brahman" thus created by constant practice destroys ignorance and the agitation caused by it, just as medicine or Rasayana destroys disease.

bhāvayedekamātmānaṃ tamanantamananyadhīḥ || 38 ||

**Translation**

## Verse 39

**IAST**

bhāvayedekamātmānaṃ nirmalākāśavatsadā || 39 ||

**Translation**

The wise one should intelligently merge the entire world-of-objects in the Atman alone and constantly think of the Self ever as contaminated by anything as the sky.

## Verse 40

**Sanskrit**

रूपवर्णादिकं सर्वं विहाय परमार्थवित्।
परिपूर्णचिदानन्दस्वरूपेणावतिष्ठते ॥४०॥

**IAST**

rūpavarṇādikaṃ sarvaṃ vihāya paramārthavit.
paripūrṇacidānandasvarūpeṇāvatiṣṭhate || 40 ||

**Translation**

He who has realised the Supreme, discards all his identification with the objects of names and forms. (Thereafter) he dwells as an embodiment of the Infinite Consciousness and Bliss. He becomes the Self.
