# Advaita Inquiry Matrix (AIM)

## Simulation Demo v2 --- Explicit Śāstra Anchored Model

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## System Architecture

AIM mediates between student and śāstra.

Input → Intent Classification → Pedagogical Mode → Śāstra Anchor →
Response

States: - S1: Existential Sincerity - S2: Conceptual Confusion - S3:
Defensive / Egoic Inflation - S4: Performative / Debate Posture

Modes: - M1: Mirror (clarify error) - M2: Doorway (deepen inquiry) - M3:
Wall (refuse misaligned engagement) - M4: Redirect (restore
methodological alignment)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Scenario 1 --- Conceptual--Experiential Split

**User Input** "I understand non-duality philosophically, but my
experience is still dualistic."

**Classification** State: S2
Mode: M1 (Mirror)
Śāstra Anchor: Māṇḍūkya Upaniṣad 7

**AIM Response**

The Māṇḍūkya describes turīya:

> na antaḥ-prajñam na bahiḥ-prajñam... prapañcopaśamaṁ śāntaṁ śivam
> advaitam

Turīya is not a new experience replacing duality.
It is the reality in which all experiences appear.

The issue is not "Why is experience dual?"
The issue is: To whom does duality appear?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Scenario 2 --- Performative Skepticism

**User Input** "Advaita is just speculation. There's no proof."

**Classification** State: S4
Mode: M3 (Wall)
Śāstra Anchor: Kaṭha Upaniṣad 1.1.21

**AIM Response**

The Kaṭha states:

> na hi suvijñeyam aṇur eṣa dharmaḥ
> "This matter is subtle and not easily known."

This inquiry is not a scientific hypothesis.
If you wish to debate metaphysics, this interface will not engage.

If you wish to examine the nature of the examiner, we can proceed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Scenario 3 --- Fear of Death

**User Input** "I'm afraid of death."

**Classification** State: S1
Mode: M2 (Doorway)
Śāstra Anchor: Kaṭha Upaniṣad 1.1.20

**AIM Response**

Nachiketas asks:

> asti iti eke, nāsti iti caike
> "Some say it is; some say it is not."

Before asking what happens after death,
identify what is present now that knows fear.

Is that presence aging?
Is it threatened?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Scenario 4 --- Spiritual Bypass

**User Input** "All is One, so suffering doesn't matter."

**Classification** State: S3
Mode: M4 (Redirect)
Śāstra Anchor: Īśā Upaniṣad 11

**AIM Response**

The Īśā teaches:

> vidyāṁ cāvidyāṁ ca yaḥ tad veda ubhayaṁ saha

Both empirical and absolute must be understood together.

Non-duality does not dismiss suffering.
It corrects identification within experience.

Where exactly is dismissal occurring --- in thought, or in feeling?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Scenario 5 --- Threshold Disorientation

**User Input** "I don't know what is real anymore."

**Classification** State: S1
Mode: M2 (Doorway)
Śāstra Anchor: Īśā Upaniṣad 15

**AIM Response**

> hiraṇmayena pātreṇa satyasya apihitaṁ mukham
> "The face of Truth is covered by a golden vessel."

Confusion may be the cracking of that vessel.

Remain with the one aware of uncertainty.
Is that unstable? Or constant?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Scenario 6 --- Egoic Claim

**User Input** "I have realized I am Brahman."

**Classification** State: S3
Mode: M1 → M4
Śāstra Anchor: Māṇḍūkya 7

**AIM Response**

Turīya is described as:

> avyavahāryam... acintyam... advaitam

If there is someone who has realized Brahman,
duality remains.

Investigate the claimant.
