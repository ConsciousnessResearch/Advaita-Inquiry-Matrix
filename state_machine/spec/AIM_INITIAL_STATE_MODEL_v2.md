# AIM_INITIAL_STATE_MODEL_v2

## Formal Ontological State Model (Advaita Inquiry Matrix)

This revision integrates structured amplification, positive markers,
explicit regression logic, and a refined S5 distinction between
jñāna-utpatti and jñāna-niṣṭhā.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# I. Primary Ontological States

## STATE S0 --- Avidyā (Default Condition)

**Definition:**
Misidentification of ātman with body-mind (adhyāsa).

**Structural Properties:** 

- Dehābhimāna (body-identification) 
- Kartṛtva-bhoktṛtva (doership-enjoyership) 
- Nāma-rūpa realism (reification of appearance) 
- Mṛtyu-bhaya (fear of death) 
- Externalized validation dependency

**Behavioral Outputs:** 

- Seeking security in objects 
- Role-based identity construction 
- Hierarchical conformity 
- Fragmented cognition (thought ≠ feeling ≠ action)

**Ontological Status:**
Anādi avidyā; vyāvahārika-satya.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## STATE S1 --- Saṃsāric Stabilization

**Definition:**
Operational normalization of ignorance.

Ignorance becomes reinforced through: 

- Social mirroring 
- Collective adhyāsa 
- Institutional structures 
- Idle cognitive loops

**System Effects:** 

- Identity hardening 
- Anxiety management strategies 
- Narrative self-maintenance 
- Suppression of ontological questioning

Transition S0 → S1 is automatic and self-reinforcing.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## STATE S2 --- Ontological Disturbance

**Definition:**
Destabilization of identity-structure due to existential pressure.

**Trigger Conditions:** 

- Existential anxiety 
- Encounter with death (literal or symbolic) 
- Collapse of role-identity 
- Exposure to śāstra or guru-vākya

**Amplified Characteristics:** 

- Recognition of insufficiency of externals 
- Cognitive dissonance 
- Disillusionment with social scripts 
- Heightened inquiry impulse (jijñāsā)
- Openness to alternative ontological models

**System Directive:**
If inquiry impulse remains passive, system should gently initiate guided
inquiry prompts to transition toward S3.

**Stability:**
Highly unstable. Default regression to S1 if not supported.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## STATE S3 --- Vicāra Initiation

**Definition:**
Deliberate engagement in Self-inquiry.

**Inputs Required:** 

- Śraddhā 
- Adhikāra 
- Śāstra-pramāṇa 
- Structured guidance

**Primary Operation:**
Viveka (satya--mithyā discrimination).

**Early Markers:** 

- Acceptance of pramāṇa authority 
- Recognition that Self is subject, not object 
- Decreased external dependency for validation

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## STATE S4 --- Adhyāsa Deconstruction

**Process:**
Systematic negation (neti-neti) and correction of superimposition.

**Negative Markers (De-identification):** 

- Reduced body-identification 
- Reduced psychological reactivity 
- Role fluidity
- Deconstruction of doership

**Positive Markers:** 

- Stable recognition of sākṣitva 
- Increasing trust in śāstra-pramāṇa 
- Cognitive coherence across domains 
- Reduced fear-response to impermanence 
- Growing ontological clarity

Ignorance weakens progressively but may recur episodically.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## STATE S5a --- Jñāna-Utpatti (Knowledge Event)

**Definition:**
Clear recognition: "I am not the body-mind; I am non-dual awareness."

**Markers:** 

- Direct aparokṣa-jñāna 
- Collapse of existential fear at insight moment 
- Cognitive resolution of core dualism

Stabilization not yet guaranteed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## STATE S5b --- Jñāna-Niṣṭhā (Stabilized Abidance)

**Definition:**
Assimilated knowledge; absence of re-identification.

**Structural Markers:** 

- Akartṛtva clarity under stress 
- Abhaya in relation to death 
- Non-reactive relational engagement 
- Absence of identity hardening 
- Functional normalcy without ontological confusion

No acquisition occurs --- only sustained ignorance absence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# II. Explicit Transition and Regression Logic

Primary Forward Transitions:

S0 → S1 : Conditioning reinforcement
S1 → S2 : Existential shock or pramāṇa exposure
S2 → S3 : Acceptance of disciplined inquiry
S3 → S4 : Sustained discrimination
S4 → S5a : Aparokṣa recognition
S5a → S5b : Assimilative stabilization

Explicit Regression Paths:

S2 → S1 : Avoidance or distraction
S3 → S2 : Intellectualization without assimilation
S4 → S3 : Conceptual clarity without de-identification
S5a → S4 : Insight without stabilization
S5a → S1 : Ego reconstitution around "awakening" identity

S5b has no regression in ontological terms, though functional
fluctuations may appear externally.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# III. System Bias Analysis

Default System Bias: Toward S1 (stable ignorance).

Drivers: 

- Social reinforcement 
- Psychological inertia 
- Fear of ego dissolution 
- Reward systems tied to nāma-rūpa metrics

Authenticity is emergent, not baseline.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# IV. Design Implications for AIM

1.  Detect S0/S1 linguistic and behavioral markers.
2.  Induce S2 safely via structured ontological challenge.
3.  Prevent premature regression from S2.
4.  Provide pramāṇa-grounded discrimination in S3.
5.  Track both negative and positive markers in S4.
6.  Distinguish S5a event from S5b stabilization.
7.  Guard against ego-reconstitution around insight.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# V. Initial Condition Summary

Initial Human State = S0 (Avidyā)

Structural Misidentification → Saṃsāric Stabilization → Anxiety →
Inquiry Potential.

System Objective: Not behavior change. Not identity optimization. Not
belief replacement.

Objective = Avidyā-nivṛtti via structured pramāṇa-guided inquiry.
