### Mantra 1

**Sanskrit**

ओं सहनावितिशान्तिः । ओं उशन्ह वै वाजश्रवसः सर्ववेदसंददौ । 
तस्य ह नचिकेता नाम पुत्र आस ॥ १ ॥

**Transliteration**

oṃ sahanāvitiśāntiḥ | oṃ uśanha vai vājaśravasaḥ sarvavedasaṃdadau | 
tasya ha naciketā nāma putra āsa || 1 ||

**Translation**

From desire, as story tells, the son of Vajasrava made a gift of all his wealth; he had a son, so the story goes, by name Nachiketas.

### Mantra 3

**Sanskrit**

**Transliteration**

**Translation**

(These cows) have drunk water for the last time, eaten grass for the last time, have yielded all their milk and are devoid of vigour. Joyless verily are those worlds; them he attains who gives these.

**Transliteration**

**Translation**

Hope and expectation, company with good men, true and pleasant discourse, sacrifices, acts of pious liberality, children and cattle, all these are destroyed in the case of the ignorant man, in whose house a *Brahman* guest fasting stays.

**Translation**

**Translation**

### Mantra 17

**Sanskrit**

त्रिणाचिकेतस्त्रिभिरेत्य सन्धिं त्रिकर्मकृत्तरति जन्ममृत्यू ।

**Transliteration**

triṇāciketastribhiretya sandhiṃ trikarmakṛttarati janmamṛtyū । 
brahmajajñaṃ devamīḍyaṃ viditvā nicāyyemām̐ śāntimatyantameti ॥ 17 ॥

**Translation**

The three-fold *Nachiketas*, being united with the three doing, the three-fold *Karma*, crosses birth and death, knowing the adorable, the bright, the omniscient fire born of *Brahman* and realising him, attains thorough peace.

### Mantra 25

**Sanskrit**

ये ये कामा दुर्लभा मर्त्यलोके सर्वान्कामाँश्छन्दतः प्रार्थयस्व ।

आभिर्मत्प्रत्ताभिः परिचारयस्व नचिकेतो मरणं मानुप्राक्शीः ॥ २५ ॥

**Transliteration**

ye ye kāmā durlabhā martyaloke sarvānkāmām̐śchandataḥ prārthayasva |

**Translation**

Whatever desires are difficult to realise in the land of mortals, ask, as thou likest, for all such desired objects. These nymphs have their chariots and lutes; and women like these are not enjoyable by mortals; with these, by me given, have thy services performed. Oh *Nachiketas*, do not ask about death.

### Mantra 26

**Sanskrit**

**Translation**

(*Nachiketas* says) Ephemeral these; Oh Death, these tend to the decay of the fire (vigour) of all the senses in man. Even the longest life is, indeed, short. Thine alone be the chariots, the dance and music.

### Mantra 27

**Sanskrit**

न वित्तेन तर्पणीयो मनुष्यो लप्स्यामहे वित्तमद्राक्श्म चेत्त्वां । 
जीविष्यामो यावदीशिष्यसि त्वं वरस्तु मे वरणीयः स एव ॥ २७ ॥

**Transliteration**

na vittena tarpaṇīyo manuṣyo lapsyāmahe vittamadrākśma cettvāṃ | 
jīviṣyāmo yāvadīśiṣyasi tvaṃ varastu me varaṇīyaḥ sa eva || 27 ||

**Translation**

Man is not to be satisfied with wealth; if wealth were wanted, we shall get it, if we only see thee. We shall also live, as long as you rule. Therefore, that boon alone is fit to be craved by me.

### Mantra 28

**Sanskrit**

अजीर्यताममृतानामुपेत्य जीर्यन्मर्त्यः क्वधःस्थः प्रजानन् । 
अभिध्यायन्वर्णरतिप्रमोदानतिदीर्घे जीविते को रमेत ॥ २८ ॥

**Transliteration**

ajīryatāmamṛtānāmupetya jīryanmartyaḥ kvadhaḥsthaḥ prajānan | 
abhidhyāyanvarṇaratipramodānatidīrghe jīvite ko rameta || 28 ||

**Translation**

What decaying mortal living in the world below and possessed of knowledge, having reached the company of the undecaying and the immortal, will delight in long life, knowing the nature of the delight produced by song and sport?

### Mantra 29

**Sanskrit**

यस्मिन्निदं विचिकित्सन्ति मृत्यो यत्साम्पराये महति ब्रूहि नस्तत् । 
योऽयं वरो गूढमनुप्रविष्टो नान्यं तस्मान्नचिकेता वृणीते ॥ २९ ॥

**Transliteration**

yasminnidaṃ vicikitsanti mṛtyo yatsāmparāye mahati brūhi nastat | 
yo'yaṃ varo gūḍhamanupraviṣṭo nānyaṃ tasmānnaciketā vṛṇīte || 29 ||

**Translation**

Oh Death, tell us that in which men have this doubt, and which is about the great hereafter; no other boon doth *Nachiketas* crave, than this which entered into the secret.
