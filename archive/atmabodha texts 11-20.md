## Verse 11

**Sanskrit**

नानोपाधिवशादेव जातिनामाश्रमादयः।
आत्मन्यारोपितास्तोये रसवर्णादिभेदवत् ॥११॥

**IAST**

nānopādhivaśādeva jātināmāśramādayaḥ.
ātmanyāropitāstoye rasavarṇādibhedavat || 1 ||

**Translation**

Because of Its association with different conditionings (Upadhis) such ideas as caste, colour and position are super-imposed upon the Atman, as flavour, colour, etc., are super-imposed on water.

## Verse 12

**Sanskrit**

पञ्चीकृतमहाभूतसंभवं कर्मसंचितम्।
शरीरं सुखदुःखानां भोगायतनमुच्यते ॥१२॥

**IAST**

pañcīkṛtamahābhūtasaṃbhavaṃ karmasaṃcitam.
śarīraṃ sukhaduḥkhānāṃ bhogāyatanamucyate || 1 ||

**Translation**

Determined for each individual by his own past actions and made up of the Five elements - that have gone through the process of "five-fold self-division and mutual combination" (pañcīkaraṇa) - are born the gross-body, the medium through which pleasure and pain are experienced, the tent-of-experiences.

---

## Verse 13

**Sanskrit**

पञ्चप्राणमनोबुद्धिदशेन्द्रियसमन्वितम्।
अपञ्चीकृतभूतोत्थं सूक्ष्माङ्गं भोगसाधनम् ॥१३॥

**IAST**

pañcaprāṇamanobuddhidaśendriyasamanvitam.
apañcīkṛtabhūtotthaṃ sūkṣmāṅgaṃ bhogasādhanam || 1 ||

**Translation**

The five Pranas, the ten organs and the Manas and the Buddhi, formed from the rudimentary elements (Tanmatras) before their "five-fold division and mutual combination with one another" (pañcīkaraṇa) and this is the subtle body, the instruments-of-experience (of the individual).

---

## Verse 14

**Sanskrit**

अनाद्यविद्यानिर्वाच्या कारणोपाधिरुच्यते।
उपाधित्रितयादन्यमात्मानमवधारयेत् ॥१४॥

**IAST**

anādyavidyānirvācyā kāraṇopādhirucyate.
upādhitritayādanyamātmānamavadhārayet || 1 ||

**Translation**

Avidya which is indescribable and beginningless is the Causal Body. Know for certain that the Atman is other than these three conditioning bodies (Upadhis).

---

## Verse 15

**Sanskrit**

पञ्चकोशादियोगेन तत्तन्मय इव स्थितः।
शुद्धात्मा नीलवस्त्रादियोगेन स्फटिको यथा ॥१५॥

**IAST**

pañcakośādiyogena tattanmaya iva sthitaḥ.
śuddhātmā nīlavastrādiyogena sphaṭiko yathā || 1 ||

**Translation**

In its identification with the five-sheaths the Immaculate Atman appears to have borrowed their qualities upon Itself; as in the case of a crystal which appears to gather unto itself colour of its vicinity (blue cloth, etc.,).

---

## Verse 16

**Sanskrit**

वपुस्तुषादिभिः कोशैर्युक्तं युक्त्यवघाततः।
आत्मानमान्तरं शुद्धं विविञ्च्यात्तण्डुलं यथा ॥१६॥

**IAST**

vapustuṣādibhiḥ kośairyuktaṃ yuktyavaghātataḥ.
ātmānamāntaraṃ śuddhaṃ viviñcyāttaṇḍulaṃ yathā || 1 ||

**Translation**

Through discriminative self-analysis and logical thinking one should separate the Pure self within from the sheaths as one separates the rice from the husk, bran, etc., that are covering it.

---

## Verse 17

**Sanskrit**

सदा सर्वगतोऽप्यात्मा न सर्वत्रावभासते।
बुद्धावेवावभासेत स्वच्छेषु प्रतिबिम्बवत् ॥१७॥

**IAST**

sadā sarvagato'pyātmā na sarvatrāvabhāsate.
buddhāvevāvabhāseta svaccheṣu pratibimbavat || 1 ||

**Translation**

The Atman does not shine in everything although He is All-pervading. He is manifest only in the inner equipment, the intellect (Buddhi): just as the reflection in a clean mirror.

---

## Verse 18

**Sanskrit**

देहेन्द्रियमनोबुद्धिप्रकृतिभ्यो विलक्षणम्।
तद्वृत्तिसाक्षिणं विद्यादात्मानं राजवत्सदा ॥१८॥

**IAST**

dehendriyamanobuddhiprakṛtibhyo vilakṣaṇam.
tadvṛttisākṣiṇaṃ vidyādātmānaṃ rājavatsadā || 1 ||

**Translation**

One should understand that the Atman is always like the King, distinct from the body, senses, mind and intellect, all of which constitute the matter (Prakriti); and is the witness of their functions.

---

## Verse 19

**Sanskrit**

व्यापृतेष्विन्द्रियेष्वात्मा व्यापारीवाविवेकिनाम्।
दृश्यतेऽभ्रेषु धावत्सु धावन्निव यथा शशी ॥१९॥

**IAST**

vyāpṛteṣvindriyeṣvātmā vyāpārīvāvivekinām.
dṛśyate'bhreṣu dhāvatsu dhāvanniva yathā śaśī || 1 ||

**Translation**

The moon appears to be running when the clouds move in the sky. Likewise to the non-discriminating person the Atman appears to be active when It is observed through the functions of the sense-organs.

---

## Verse 20

**Sanskrit**

आत्मचैतन्यमाश्रित्य देहेन्द्रियमनोधियः।
स्वकीयार्थेषु वर्तन्ते सूर्यालोकं यथा जनाः ॥२०॥

**IAST**

ātmacaitanyamāśritya dehendriyamanodhiyaḥ.
svakīyārtheṣu vartante sūryālokaṃ yathā janāḥ || 1 ||

**Translation**

Depending upon the energy of vitality of Consciousness (Atma Chaitanya) the body, senses, mind and intellect engage themselves in their respective activities, just as men work depending upon the light of the Sun.
