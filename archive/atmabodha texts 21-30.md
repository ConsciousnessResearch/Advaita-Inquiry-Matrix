## Verse 21

**Sanskrit**

देहेन्द्रियगुणान्कर्माण्यमले सच्चिदात्मनि।
अध्यस्यन्त्यविवेकेन गगने नीलतादिवत् ॥२१॥

**IAST**

dehendriyaguṇānkarmāṇyamale saccidātmani.
adhyasyantyavivekena gagane nīlatādivat || 1 ||

**Translation**

Fools, because they lack in their powers of discrimination superimpose on the Atman, the Absolute-Existence-Knowledge (Sat-Chit), all the varied functions of the body and the senses, just as they attribute blue colour and the like to the sky.

## Verse 22

**Sanskrit**

अज्ञानान्मानसोपाधेः कर्तृत्वादीनि चात्मनि।
कल्प्यन्तेऽम्बुगते चन्द्रे चलनादि यथाम्भसः ॥२२॥

**IAST**

ajñānānmānasopādheḥ kartṛtvādīni cātmani.
kalpyante'mbugate candre calanādi yathāmbhasaḥ || 1 ||

**Translation**

The tremblings that belong to the waters are attributed through ignorance to the reflected moon dancing on it: likewise agency of action, of enjoyment and of other limitations (which really belong to the mind) are delusively understood as the nature of the Self (Atman).

## Verse 23

**Sanskrit**

रागेच्छासुखदुःखादि बुद्धौ सत्यां प्रवर्तते।
सुषुप्तौ नास्ति तन्नाशे तस्माद्बुद्धेस्तु नात्मनः ॥२३॥

**IAST**

rāgecchāsukhaduḥkhādi buddhau satyāṃ pravartate.
suṣuptau nāsti tannāśe tasmādbuddhestu nātmanaḥ || 1 ||

**Translation**

Attachment, desire, pleasure, pain, etc., are perceived to exist so long as Buddhi or mind functions. They are not perceived in deep sleep when the mind ceases to exist. Therefore they belong to the mind alone and not to the Atman.

## Verse 24

**Sanskrit**

प्रकाशोऽर्कस्य तोयस्य शैत्यमग्नेर्यथोष्णता।
स्वभावः सच्चिदानन्दनित्यनिर्मलतात्मनः ॥२४॥

**IAST**

prakāśo'rkasya toyasya śaityamagneryathoṣṇatā.
svabhāvaḥ saccidānandanityanirmalatātmanaḥ || 1 ||

**Translation**

Just as luminosity is the nature of the Sun, coolness of water and heat of fire, so too the nature of the Atman is Eternity, Purity, Reality, Consciousness and Bliss.

## Verse 25

**Sanskrit**

आत्मनः सच्चिदंशश्च बुद्धेर्वृत्तिरिति द्वयम्।
संयोज्य चाविवेकेन जानामीति प्रवर्तते ॥२५॥

**IAST**

ātmanaḥ saccidaṃśaśca buddhervṛttiriti dvayam.
saṃyojya cāvivekena jānāmīti pravartate || 1 ||

**Translation**

By the indiscriminate blending of the two - the Existence-Knowledge-aspect of the Self and the thought-wave of the intellect - there arises the notion of "I know".

## Verse 26

**Sanskrit**

आत्मनो विक्रिया नास्ति बुद्धेर्बोधो न जात्विति।
जीवः सर्वमलं ज्ञात्वा ज्ञाता द्रष्टेति मुह्यति ॥२६॥

**IAST**

ātmano vikriyā nāsti buddherbodho na jātviti.
jīvaḥ sarvamalaṃ jñātvā jñātā draṣṭeti muhyati || 1 ||

**Translation**

Atman never does anything and the intellect of its own accord has no capacity to experience'I know'. But the individuality in us delusorily thinks he is himself the seer and the knower.

## Verse 27

**Sanskrit**

रज्जुसर्पवदात्मानं जीवं ज्ञात्वा भयं वहेत्।
नाहं जीवः परात्मेति ज्ञातश्चेन्निर्भयो भवेत् ॥२७॥

**IAST**

rajjusarpavadātmānaṃ jīvaṃ jñātvā bhayaṃ vahet.
nāhaṃ jīvaḥ parātmeti jñātaścennirbhayo bhavet || 1 ||

**Translation**

Just as the person who regards a rope as a snake is overcome by fear, so also one considering oneself as the ego (Jiva) is overcome by fear. The ego-centric individuality in us regains fearlessness by realising that It is not a Jiva but is Itself the Supreme Soul.

---

## Verse 28

**Sanskrit**

आत्मावभासयत्येको बुद्ध्यादीनीन्द्रियाणि हि।
दीपो घटादिवत्स्वात्मा जडैस्तैर्नावभास्यते ॥२८॥

**IAST**

ātmāvabhāsayatyeko buddhyādīnīndriyāṇi hi.
dīpo ghaṭādivatsvātmā jaḍaistairnāvabhāsyate || 1 ||

**Translation**

Just as a lamp illumines a jar or a pot, so also the Atman illumines the mind and the sense organs, etc. These material-objects by themselves cannot illumine themselves because they are inert.

---

## Verse 29

**Sanskrit**

स्वबोधे नान्यबोधेच्छा बोधरूपतयात्मनः।
न दीपस्यान्यदीपेच्छा यथा स्वात्मप्रकाशने ॥२९॥

**IAST**

svabodhe nānyabodhecchā bodharūpatayātmanaḥ.
na dīpasyānyadīpecchā yathā svātmaprakāśane || 1 ||

**Translation**

A lighted lamp does not need another lamp to illumine its light. So too, Atman which is Knowledge itself needs no other knowledge to know it.

---

## Verse 30

**Sanskrit**

निषिध्य निखिलोपाधीन्नेति नेतीति वाक्यतः।
विद्यादैक्यं महावाक्यैर्जीवात्मपरमात्मनोः ॥३०॥

**IAST**

niṣidhya nikhilopādhīnneti netīti vākyataḥ.
vidyādaikyaṃ mahāvākyairjīvātmaparamātmanoḥ || 1 ||

**Translation**

By a process of negation of the conditionings (Upadhis) through the help of the scriptural statement'It is not this, It is not this', the oneness of the individual soul and the Supreme Soul, as indicated by the great Mahavakyas, has to be realised.
