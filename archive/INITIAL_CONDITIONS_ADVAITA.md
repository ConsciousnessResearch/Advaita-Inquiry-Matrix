# INITIAL_CONDITIONS_ADVAITA

## The Default Human Condition (Reframed in Advaita Vedānta)

### 1. Mūlāvasthā: The Condition of Avidyā

The default human condition is **avidyā** (ignorance of one's true
nature as ātman-Brahman).\
This ignorance is not merely intellectual error but existential
misidentification (**adhyāsa**).

We take ourselves to be: - The body (deha) - The mind (manas) - The
personality (ahaṅkāra) - The social identity (nāma--rūpa within
vyavahāra)

This superimposition (adhyāsa) constitutes the initial condition of
embodied existence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2. Thrownness as Prārabdha-Kṣetra

What existential philosophy calls "thrownness," Advaita recognizes as
embodiment within a field structured by **prārabdha-karma**.

We do not choose: - Our birth circumstances - Our body - Our
conditioning - Our cultural world

We awaken already situated within **vyavahāra**, the transactional
domain of nāma--rūpa (name and form).

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 3. Inauthenticity as Dehābhimāna

The default bias is **dehābhimāna** --- identification with the
body-mind complex.

This manifests as: - Seeking validation through others - Defining
oneself by role and function - Measuring worth by having, doing, or
knowing - Fear of loss and death

This is bondage (bandha).

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 4. Idle Talk as Saṃsāric Vṛtti-Flow

What was described phenomenologically as idle talk, curiosity, and
ambiguity can be interpreted as:

-   **Asat-vṛtti** -- mental movements not grounded in Self-knowledge\
-   **Saṃsāric distraction** -- outward-turning consciousness
    (bahirmukhatva)\
-   **Collective adhyāsa** -- socially reinforced misidentification

The world perpetuates ignorance through constant reinforcement of
dualistic narratives.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 5. Dualism as Fundamental Error

The assumption "I am here, the world is out there" is the basic error.

This dualism (dvaita-darśana) produces: - Competition with the world -
Anxiety about survival - Objectification of self and others -
Instrumental relationships

From the standpoint of Advaita, this is a misreading of reality.\
**Brahman alone is.**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 6. Anxiety as Mṛtyu-Bhaya

When temporality is introduced, existential anxiety becomes
**mṛtyu-bhaya** --- fear of death.

Because the Self is mistaken for the body, the natural dissolution of
the body is interpreted as annihilation of the self.

This produces: - Chronic insecurity - Defensive identity construction -
Obsession with continuity through legacy, possession, or recognition

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 7. Fragmentation of Being

Human functions --- thinking, feeling, doing, knowing --- are mistaken
for the Self.

Advaita clarifies: - These are modifications (vṛtti) of antaḥkaraṇa. -
They belong to the subtle body (sūkṣma-śarīra). - They are witnessed by
ātman.

Confusion between witness (sākṣin) and instrument produces
fragmentation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 8. Structural Bias Toward Saṃsāra

Saṃsāra is not accidental; it is structurally sustained by ignorance.

The world (jagat) continuously reinforces: - Identification with
nāma--rūpa - External validation - Hierarchical objectification -
Comparison and competition

Thus the tendency toward "falling" is simply the momentum of avidyā.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 9. Authenticity as Ātma-Svarūpa-Pratyabhijñā

Authenticity, in Advaita terms, is recognition (pratyabhijñā) of one's
true nature as:

-   Sākṣin (witness-consciousness)
-   Asaṅga (unattached)
-   Akartā (non-doer)
-   Abhoktā (non-experiencer in the ultimate sense)
-   Sat-cit-ānanda

This is not acquisition of something new but removal of ignorance
(avidyā-nivṛtti).

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Summary of Initial Conditions (Advaita Frame)

The default human condition is:

-   Avidyā (ignorance of true nature)
-   Adhyāsa (superimposition of body-mind upon Self)
-   Dehābhimāna (identification with the finite)
-   Saṃsāra (cyclical entanglement in nāma--rūpa)
-   Mṛtyu-bhaya (fear of death)
-   Fragmentation through misidentification with functions
-   Social reinforcement of duality

Authenticity corresponds to Self-knowledge (ātma-jñāna).\
It is not constructed but revealed through inquiry (vicāra) and
discrimination (viveka).

The initial condition is bondage.\
The solution is knowledge.
