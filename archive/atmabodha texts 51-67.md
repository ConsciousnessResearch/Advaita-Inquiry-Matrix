## Verse 51

**Sanskrit**

बाह्यानित्यसुखासक्तिं हित्वात्मसुखनिर्वृतः।
घटस्थदीपवच्छश्वदन्तरेव प्रकाशते ॥५१॥

**IAST**

bāhyānityasukhāsaktiṃ hitvātmasukhanirvṛtaḥ.
ghaṭasthadīpavacchaśvadantareva prakāśate || 51 ||

**Translation**

The self-abiding Jivan Mukta, relinquishing all his attachments to the illusory external happiness and satisfied with the bliss derived from the Atman, shines inwardly like a lamp placed inside a jar.

---

## Verse 52

**Sanskrit**

उपाधिस्थोऽपि तद्धर्मैरलिप्तो व्योमवन्मुनिः।
सर्वविन्मूढवत्तिष्ठेदसक्तो वायुवच्चरेत् ॥५२॥

**IAST**

upādhistho'pi taddharmairalipto vyomavanmuniḥ.
sarvavinmūḍhavattiṣṭhedasakto vāyuvaccaret || 52 ||

**Translation**

Though he lives in the conditionings (Upadhis), he, the contemplative one, remains ever unconcerned with anything or he may move about like the wind, perfectly unattached.

---

## Verse 53

**Sanskrit**

उपाधिविलयाद्विष्णौ निर्विशेषं विशेन्मुनिः।
जले जलं वियद्व्योम्नि तेजस्तेजसि वा यथा ॥५३॥

**IAST**

upādhivilayādviṣṇau nirviśeṣaṃ viśenmuniḥ.
jale jalaṃ viyadvyomni tejastejasi vā yathā || 53 ||

**Translation**

On the destruction of the Upadhis, the contemplative one is totally absorbed in'Vishnu', the All-pervading Spirit, like water into water, space into space and light into light.

---

## Verse 54

**Sanskrit**

यल्लाभान्नापरो लाभो यत्सुखान्नापरं सुखम्।
यज्ज्ञानान्नापरं ज्ञानं तद्ब्रह्मेत्यवधारयेत् ॥५४॥

**IAST**

yallābhānnāparo lābho yatsukhānnāparaṃ sukham.
yajjñānānnāparaṃ jñānaṃ tadbrahmetyavadhārayet || 54 ||

**Translation**

Realise That to be Brahman, the attainment of which leaves nothing more to be attained, the blessedness of which leaves no other blessing to be desired and the knowledge of which leaves nothing more to be known.

---

## Verse 55

**Sanskrit**

यद्दृष्ट्वा नापरं दृश्यं यद्भूत्वा न पुनर्भवः।
यज्ज्ञात्वा नापरं ज्ञेयं तद्ब्रह्मेत्यवधारयेत् ॥५५॥

**IAST**

yaddṛṣṭvā nāparaṃ dṛśyaṃ yadbhūtvā na punarbhavaḥ.
yajjñātvā nāparaṃ jñeyaṃ tadbrahmetyavadhārayet || 55 ||

**Translation**

Realise that to be Brahman which, when seen, leaves nothing more to be seen, which having become one is not born again in this world and which, when knowing leaves nothing else to be known.

---

## Verse 56

**Sanskrit**

तिर्यगूर्ध्वमधः पूर्णं सच्चिदानन्दमद्वयम्।
अनन्तं नित्यमेकं यत्तद्ब्रह्मेत्यवधारयेत् ॥५६॥

**IAST**

tiryagūrdhvamadhaḥ pūrṇaṃ saccidānandamadvayam.
anantaṃ nityamekaṃ yattadbrahmetyavadhārayet || 56 ||

**Translation**

Realise that to be Brahman which is Existence-Knowledge-Bliss-Absolute, which is Non-dual, Infinite, Eternal and One and which fills all the quarters - above and below and all that exists between.

---

## Verse 57

**Sanskrit**

अतद्व्यावृत्तिरूपेण वेदान्तैर्लक्ष्यतेऽव्ययम्।
अखण्डानन्दमेकं यत्तद्ब्रह्मेत्यवधारयेत् ॥५७॥

**IAST**

atadvyāvṛttirūpeṇa vedāntairlakṣyate'vyayam.
akhaṇḍānandamekaṃ yattadbrahmetyavadhārayet || 57 ||

**Translation**

Realise that to be Brahman which is Non-dual, Indivisible, One and Blissful and which is indicated in Vedanta as the Immutable Substratum, realised after the negation of all tangible objects.

---

## Verse 58

**Sanskrit**

अखण्डानन्दरूपस्य तस्यानन्दलवाश्रिताः।
ब्रह्माद्यास्तारतम्येन भवन्त्यानन्दिनोऽखिलाः ॥५८॥

**IAST**

akhaṇḍānandarūpasya tasyānandalavāśritāḥ.
brahmādyāstāratamyena bhavantyānandino'khilāḥ || 58 ||

**Translation**

Deities like Brahma and others taste only a particle, of the unlimited Bliss of Brahman and enjoy in proportion their share of that particle.

---

## Verse 59

**Sanskrit**

तद्युक्तमखिलं वस्तु व्यवहारश्चिदन्वितः।
तस्मात्सर्वगतं ब्रह्म क्षीरे सर्पिरिवाखिले ॥५९॥

**IAST**

tadyuktamakhilaṃ vastu vyavahāraścidanvitaḥ.
tasmātsarvagataṃ brahma kṣīre sarpirivākhile || 59 ||

**Translation**

All objects are pervaded by Brahman. All actions are possible because of Brahman: therefore Brahman permeates everything as butter permeates milk.

---

## Verse 60

**Sanskrit**

अनण्वस्थूलमह्रस्वमदीर्घमजमव्ययम्।
अरूपगुणवर्णाख्यं तद्ब्रह्मेत्यवधारयेत् ॥६०॥

**IAST**

anaṇvasthūlamahrasvamadīrghamajamavyayam.
arūpaguṇavarṇākhyaṃ tadbrahmetyavadhārayet || 60 ||

**Translation**

Realise that to be Brahman which is neither subtle nor gross: neither short nor long: without birth or change: without form, qualities, colour and name.

---

## Verse 61

**Sanskrit**

यद्भासा भास्यतेऽर्कादि भास्यैर्यत्तु न भास्यते।
येन सर्वमिदं भाति तद्ब्रह्मेत्यवधारयेत् ॥६१॥

**IAST**

yadbhāsā bhāsyate'rkādi bhāsyairyattu na bhāsyate.
yena sarvamidaṃ bhāti tadbrahmetyavadhārayet || 61 ||

**Translation**

That by the light of which the luminous, orbs like the Sun and the Moon are illuminated, but which is not illumined by their light, realise that to be Brahman.

---

## Verse 62

**Sanskrit**

स्वयमन्तर्बहिर्व्याप्य भासयन्नखिलं जगत्।
ब्रह्म प्रकाशते वह्निप्रतप्तायसपिण्डवत् ॥६२॥

**IAST**

svayamantarbahirvyāpya bhāsayannakhilaṃ jagat.
brahma prakāśate vahniprataptāyasapiṇḍavat || 62 ||

**Translation**

Pervading the entire universe outwardly and inwardly the Supreme Brahman shines of Itself like the fire that permeates a red-hot iron-ball and glows by itself.

---

## Verse 63

**Sanskrit**

जगद्विलक्षणं ब्रह्म ब्रह्मणोऽन्यन्न किंचन।
ब्रह्मान्यद्भाति चेन्मिथ्या यथा मरुमरीचिका ॥६३॥

**IAST**

jagadvilakṣaṇaṃ brahma brahmaṇo'nyanna kiṃcana.
brahmānyadbhāti cenmithyā yathā marumarīcikā || 63 ||

**Translation**

Brahman is other than this, the universe. There exists nothing that is not Brahman. If any object other than Brahman appears to exist, it is unreal like the mirage.

---

## Verse 64

**Sanskrit**

दृश्यते श्रूयते यद्यद्ब्रह्मणोऽन्यन्न तद्भवेत्।
तत्त्वज्ञानाच्च तद्ब्रह्म सच्चिदानन्दमद्वयम् ॥६४॥

**IAST**

dṛśyate śrūyate yadyadbrahmaṇo'nyanna tadbhavet.
tattvajñānācca tadbrahma saccidānandamadvayam || 64 ||

**Translation**

All that is perceived, or heard, is Brahman and nothing else. Attaining the knowledge of the Reality, one sees the Universe as the non-dual Brahman, Existence-Knowledge-Bliss-Absolute.

---

## Verse 65

**Sanskrit**

सर्वगं सच्चिदानन्दं ज्ञानचक्षुर्निरीक्षते।
अज्ञानचक्षुर्नेक्षेत भास्वन्तं भानुमन्धवत् ॥६५॥

**IAST**

sarvagaṃ saccidānandaṃ jñānacakṣurnirīkṣate.
ajñānacakṣurnekṣeta bhāsvantaṃ bhānumandhavat || 65 ||

**Translation**

Though Atman is Pure Consciousness and ever present everywhere, yet It is perceived by the eye-of-wisdom alone: but one whose vision is obscured by ignorance he does not see It; as the blind do not see the resplendent Sun.

---

## Verse 66

**Sanskrit**

श्रवणादिभिरुद्दीप्त
ज्ञानाग्निपरितापितः।
जीवः सर्वमलान्मुक्तः
स्वर्णवद्द्योतते स्वयम् ॥६६॥

**IAST**

śravaṇādibhiruddīpta
jñānāgniparitāpitaḥ.
jīvaḥ sarvamalānmuktaḥ
svarṇavaddyotate svayam || 66 ||

**Translation**

The'Jiva' free from impurities, being heated in the fire of knowledge kindled by hearing and so on, shines of itself like gold.

---

## Verse 67

**Sanskrit**

हृदाकाशोदितो ह्यात्मा
बोधभानुस्तमोपहृत्।
सर्वव्यापी सर्वधारी
भाति भासयतेऽखिलम् ॥६७॥

**IAST**

hṛdākāśodito hyātmā
bodhabhānustamopahṛt.
sarvavyāpī sarvadhārī
bhāti bhāsayate'khilam || 67 ||

**Translation**

The Atman, the Sun of Knowledge that rises in the sky of the heart, destroys the darkness of the ignorance, pervades and sustains all and shines and makes everything to shine.
