# INITIAL CONDITIONS

## The Default Human Condition (Based on *Being in the World Transcription*)

### 1. Thrownness into the World

Human beings do not begin from authenticity. We find ourselves *already*
thrown into a world structured by roles, categories, hierarchies, and
impersonal systems.\
We do not choose the initial conditions of our existence --- yet we are
required to care about and respond to them.

The world is larger, more powerful, and more complex than the
individual. As a result, the individual tends to lose themselves within
it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2. Inauthenticity as the Default State

The text argues that **inauthenticity is the structural default of human
beingness**.

This default condition includes: - Identification with externals -
Absorption in "the other" - Living according to socially imposed roles -
Mistaking social identity for real being

Authenticity is not the starting point; it must be recovered through a
deliberate phenomenological process.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 3. The Three Phenomena of Everyday Inauthenticity

Default being in the world manifests through:

-   **Idle Talk** -- Repetition of ungrounded claims, inherited
    narratives, and socially accepted interpretations of reality.
-   **Compulsive Curiosity** -- Endless distraction without depth;
    surface-level engagement.
-   **Ambiguity (Groupthink)** -- The confusion that arises when
    everyone repeats what everyone else says.

Together these create the illusion that we are living authentically when
we are merely conforming.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 4. Objectification and Impersonalism

The bias of the world is toward treating persons as objects.

-   Individuals become tools within systems.
-   Corporate and hierarchical structures reinforce objectification.
-   "Having," "doing," and "knowing" are substituted for *being*.

Impersonalism becomes the dominant mode of existence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 5. Loss of Authentic Self

In default being: - We do not see ourselves as we truly are. - Others do
not see us as we truly are. - We see ourselves through categories
imposed by others.

Energy, intelligence, and talent remain partially inaccessible because
we are not grounded in our authentic being.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 6. Dualism and the World's Dominance

A dualistic framework (self vs. world) dominates default philosophy and
culture.

Since the world is stronger than the individual, the individual
inevitably "loses" in this model. This reinforces anxiety, conformity,
and loss of self.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 7. Care, Anxiety, and Death

When temporality is introduced, death becomes the ultimate ontological
boundary.

-   Time limits possibility.
-   Death is the ultimate object of care.
-   Anxiety arises from this confrontation with finitude.

Authenticity requires integrating awareness of death into one's being,
rather than fleeing from it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 8. Integrity as Wholeness

Human being consists of parts: - Knowing - Thinking - Feeling - Doing -
Having

But none of these equal the whole. The part is not the whole.

Integrity requires integrating all dimensions into unified being. This
cannot be imposed externally; it must arise through phenomenological
self-reflection.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 9. Structural Bias Toward Falling

The tendency toward "falling" away from authenticity is existential, not
accidental.

The world tempts us constantly back into: - Conformity -
Objectification - Distraction - Social absorption

Authenticity is therefore a modification of inauthenticity, not the
starting position.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Summary of Initial Conditions

The default human condition is:

-   Thrown into an impersonal world
-   Structurally biased toward inauthenticity
-   Absorbed in idle talk, curiosity, and ambiguity
-   Objectified by hierarchical systems
-   Confusing functions (having, doing, knowing) with being
-   Anxious due to temporality and death
-   Mistakenly assuming this condition is authentic

Authenticity is not automatic. It requires deliberate phenomenological
self-reflection to recover one's original being.
