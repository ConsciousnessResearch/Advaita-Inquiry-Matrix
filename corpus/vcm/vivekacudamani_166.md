## Text 166

**Sanskrit**

नैवात्मािप प्राणमयो वायुिवकारो
गन्तागन्ता वायुवदन्तबर्िहरेषः ।
यस्माित्कं िचत्क्वािप न वेत्तीष्टमिनष्टं
स्वं वान्यं वा िकं चन िनत्यं परतन्त्रः ॥ १६६ ॥

**Transliteration**

naivātmāpi prāṇamayo vāyuvikāro
gantāgantā vāyuvadantarbahireṣaḥ |
yasmātkiṃcitkvāpi na vettīṣṭamaniṣṭaṃ
svaṃ vānyaṃ vā kiṃcana nityaṃ paratantraḥ || 166 ||

**Translation**

“This prāṇamaya-kośa can never be the Ātman. It is the effect of the wind. Like wind it goes in and comes out. As it is not aware of good or evil anywhere or at any time, either of itself or of others; and as it is dependent also, it cannot be the Ātman.“
