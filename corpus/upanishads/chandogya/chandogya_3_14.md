## Text 3.14.1

**Sanskrit**

सर्वं खल्विदं ब्रह्म तज्जलानिति शान्त उपासीत ।
अथ खलु क्रतुमयः पुरुषो यथाक्रतुरस्मिँल्लोके पुरुषो भवति
तथेतः प्रेत्य भवति स क्रतुं कुर्वीत ॥ ३.१४.१ ॥

**Transliteration**

sarvam khalv idaṃ brahma tajjalāniti śānta upāsīta |
atha khalu kratumayaḥ puruṣo yathākratur asmiṃl loke puruṣo bhavati
tathetaḥ pretya bhavati; sa kratum kurvīta ||

**Translation**

All this is indeed Brahman. From it all things are born, in it all things live, and into it all things dissolve. Therefore one should worship with tranquillity. Now, man is constituted of will. As is his will in this world, so does he become after leaving it. Let him therefore form his will accordingly.

## Text 3.14.2

**Sanskrit**

मनोमयः प्राणशरीरो भारूपः सत्यसङ्कल्प आकाशात्मा
सर्वकर्मा सर्वकामः सर्वगन्धः सर्वरसः
सर्वमिदमभ्यात्तोऽवाक्यनादर एष म आत्माऽन्तर्हृदये ।
एतद्ब्रह्मैतमितः प्रेत्याभिसंभवितास्मीति
यस्य स्यादद्धा न विचिकित्सास्तीति ।
हारुणिरुवाच ॥ ३.१४.२ ॥

**Transliteration**

manomayaḥ prāṇaśarīro bhārūpaḥ satyasaṃkalpa ākāśātmā
sarvakarmā sarvakāmaḥ sarvagandhaḥ sarvarasaḥ
sarvam idam abhyātto 'vākyam ānadaraḥ eṣa ma ātmā 'ntarhṛdaye |
etad brahmaitam itaḥ pretya abhisambhavitāsmi iti
yasya syād addhā na vicikitsāsti iti |
hāruṇir uvāca ||

**Translation**

He is constituted of mind, his body is life, his form is light, his intention is truth, his self is space. He performs all actions, has all desires, has all smells, has all tastes. He pervades all this, is without speech and without concern. This is my Self within the heart — this is Brahman. When I depart this life I shall merge into him. He who has no doubt about this — so said Āruṇi.

## Text 3.14.3

**Sanskrit**

एष म आत्माऽन्तर्हृदयेऽणीयान् व्रीहेर्वा यवाद्वा
सर्षपाद्वा श्यामाकाद्वा श्यामाकतण्डुलाद्वाप्येष म
आत्माऽन्तर्हृदये जयायान् पृथिव्या जयायानन्तरिक्षाज्
जयायान् दिवो जयायानेभ्यो लोकेभ्यः ॥ ३.१४.३ ॥

**Transliteration**

eṣa ma ātmā 'ntarhṛdaye 'ṇīyān vrīher vā yavād vā
sarṣapād vā śyāmākād vā śyāmākataṇḍulād vā;
apy eṣa ma ātmā 'ntarhṛdaye jyāyān pṛthivyā jyāyān antarikṣāj
jyāyān divo jyāyān ebhyo lokebhyaḥ ||

**Translation**

This Self within my heart is smaller than a grain of rice, smaller than a barleycorn, smaller than a mustard seed, smaller than a grain of millet, smaller than the kernel of a grain of millet. And this Self within my heart is greater than the earth, greater than the atmosphere, greater than the sky, greater than all these worlds.

## Text 3.14.4

**Sanskrit**

सर्वकर्मा सर्वकामः सर्वगन्धः सर्वरसः सर्वमिदमभ्यात्तोऽवाक्यनादर
एष म आत्माऽन्तर्हृदयेतद्ब्रह्म
तमितः प्रेत्याभिसंभवितास्मीति
यस्य स्यादद्धा न विचिकित्सास्तीति
हारुणिरुवाच ॥ ३.१४.४ ॥

**Transliteration**

sarvakarmā sarvakāmaḥ sarvagandhaḥ sarvarasaḥ sarvam idam abhyātto 'vākyam ānadaraḥ
eṣa ma ātmā 'ntarhṛdaye etad brahma
tam itaḥ pretya abhisambhavitāsmi iti
yasya syād addhā na vicikitsāsti iti
hāruṇir uvāca ||

**Translation**

Performing all actions, having all desires, having all smells, having all tastes, pervading all this, without speech and without concern — this is my Self within the heart, this is Brahman. After leaving this life I shall merge into that. He who has no doubt about this — so said Āruṇi.
