## Text 8.1.1

**Sanskrit**

ब्रह्म पुरमिदं शरीरं ब्रह्म च वाव पुरं
यो ब्रह्मपुरे वसति स क्षेत्रज्ञः ॥

**Transliteration**

brahma puram idaṃ śarīraṃ brahma ca vāva puraṃ
yo brahmapure vasati sa kṣetrajñaḥ ||

**Translation**

This body is the city of Brahman, and within it dwells the city of Brahman. He who dwells in the city of Brahman is the knower of the field.

```yaml

ontological_scope: 'dual-register'
pedagogical_stage: 'sravana'
cognitive_mode: 'analytic_contemplative'
adhikara_level: 'madhyama'
note: "The brahmapura framing — the body is the city of Brahman, preparing the listener for the dahara teaching. The kṣetrajña (knower of the field) language connects to the ātman-as-witness teaching."
```

## Text 8.1.2

**Sanskrit**

अथ यदिदमस्मिन् ब्रह्मपुरे दहरं पुण्डरीकं वेश्म
दहरोऽस्मिन्नन्तराकाशस्तस्मिन् यदन्तस्तदन्वेष्टव्यं
तद्वाव विजिज्ञासितव्यमिति ॥ ८.१.२ ॥

**Transliteration**

atha yad idam asmin brahmapure daharaṃ puṇḍarīkaṃ veśma
daharo 'smin antarākāśas tasmin yad antas tad anveṣṭavyaṃ
tad vāva vijijñāsitavyam iti ||

**Translation**

Now, within this city of Brahman there is a small lotus, a dwelling place. Within it is a small space. What is inside that is to be sought, that is indeed to be known.

```yaml
prakriya: 'dahara-vidya'
pedagogical_role: 'dahara-akasha-pointing'
ontological_scope: 'paramarathika'
pedagogical_stage: 'manana'
cognitive_mode: 'analytic_inquiry'
adhikara_level: 'uttama'
note: "The opening of the daharākāśa (small-space) teaching. The 'small lotus' is the hṛdaya (heart). The small space within it is said to be Brahman. 'Small' (dahara) is itself part of the apophatic strategy — the infinite is pointed to through the intimate and interior."
```

## Text 8.1.3

**Sanskrit**

तं चेद् ब्रूयुः यदिदमस्मिन् ब्रह्मपुरे दहरं पुण्डरीकं वेश्म
दहरोऽस्मिन्नन्तराकाशः किमेतत्र विद्यत इति
स ब्रूयात् — यावान् वा अयमाकाशस्तावानेषोऽन्तर्हृदय आकाशः
उभे अस्मिन्द्यावापृथिवी अन्तरेव समाहिते
उभावग्निश्च वायुश्च सूर्याचन्द्रमसावुभौ
विद्युन्नक्षत्राणि यच्चास्येहास्ति यच्च नास्ति
सर्वं तदस्मिन् समाहितमिति ॥ ८.१.३ ॥

**Transliteration**

taṃ ced brūyuḥ yad idam asmin brahmapure daharaṃ puṇḍarīkaṃ veśma
daharo 'smin antarākāśaḥ kim etat ra vidyata iti
sa brūyāt — yāvān vā ayam ākāśas tāvān eṣo 'ntarhṛdaya ākāśaḥ
ubhe asmin dyāvāpṛthivī antareva samāhite
ubhāv agniś ca vāyuś ca sūryācandramasāv ubhau
vidyun nakṣatrāṇi yac cāsyehāsti yac ca nāsti
sarvaṃ tad asmin samāhitam iti ||

**Translation**

If they were to say to him: 'In this city of Brahman there is a small lotus, a dwelling, and within it a small space — what is there within it to be found?' He should say: 'As large as this space (of the universe) is, so large is this space within the heart. Within it both heaven and earth are contained, both fire and wind, both sun and moon, lightning and the stars — what is here and what is not here — all that is gathered within it.'

```yaml
prakriya: 'dahara-vidya'
pedagogical_role: 'infinite-in-the-intimate'
ontological_scope: 'paramarathika'
pedagogical_stage: 'nididhyasana'
cognitive_mode: 'direct_insight'
adhikara_level: 'uttama'
note: "The paradox of the dahara teaching: the small space in the heart (antarākāśa) contains the entire universe. This is the non-dual teaching in spatial form — the ātman (as Brahman) is not 'somewhere small' but is the ground of all space."
```

## Text 8.1.5

**Sanskrit**

सर्वे च कामाः सर्वे च गन्धाः सर्वे च रसाः सर्वमिदमभ्यात्तो
'वाक्यनादर एष म आत्मान्तर्हृदय एतद् ब्रह्म एतमितः प्रेत्याभिसंभवितास्मि ॥ ८.१.५ ॥

**Transliteration**

sarve ca kāmāḥ sarve ca gandhāḥ sarve ca rasāḥ sarvam idam abhyātto
'vākyam ānadaraḥ eṣa ma ātmān tarhṛdaya etad brahma
etam itaḥ pretya abhisambhavitāsmi ||

**Translation**

All desires, all smells, all tastes — he pervades all this, without speech, without concern. This is my Self within the heart; this is Brahman. When I depart from here I shall merge into him.

```yaml
prakriya: 'atman-as-brahman'
pedagogical_role: 'mahavakya-reiteration'
ontological_scope: 'paramarathika'
pedagogical_stage: 'nididhyasana'
cognitive_mode: 'direct_insight'
adhikara_level: 'uttama'
note: "The declaration of identity restated within the dahara context — eṣa ma ātmāntarhṛdaye etad brahma. The same formula as 3.14.4, linking both sections as a pedagogical doublet."
```

## Text 8.3.2

**Sanskrit**

तद् ब्रह्म तद् अमृतम् तद् ब्रह्म सर्वाः प्रजाः

**Transliteration**

**Translation**

## Text 8.7.1

**Sanskrit**

य आत्मा अपहतपाप्मा विजरो विमृत्युर् विशोको
विजिघत्सोऽपिपासः सत्यकामः सत्यसङ्कल्पः
सोऽन्वेष्टव्यः स विजिज्ञासितव्यः ॥ ८.७.१ ॥

**Transliteration**

ya ātmā apahatapāpmā vijaro vimṛtyur viśoko
vijighatsaḥ apipāsaḥ satyakāmaḥ satyasaṃkalpaḥ
so 'nveṣṭavyaḥ sa vijijñāsitavyaḥ ||

**Translation**

The Self that is free from evil, free from old age, free from death, free from grief, free from hunger and thirst — whose desire is truth, whose will is truth — that is to be sought, that is to be known.

```yaml
prakriya: 'atman-definition'
pedagogical_role: 'inquiry-arousal'
ontological_scope: 'paramarathika'
pedagogical_stage: 'sravana'
cognitive_mode: 'analytic_contemplative'
adhikara_level: 'madhyama'
note: "Prajāpati's opening proclamation that draws Indra and Virocana from the gods and demons respectively. The eight negations (apahatapāpmā etc.) define the ātman apophatically — what it is NOT — before any positive teaching is given."
```

**Transliteration**

ya eṣo 'kṣiṇi puruṣo dṛśyata eṣa ātmeti hovāca

**Translation**

The person seen in the eye — he is the Self, said Prajāpati. He is the immortal, the fearless; he is Brahman.

## Text 8.9.3

**Sanskrit**

मघवन् मर्त्यं वा इदं शरीरमात्तं मृत्युना
तदस्यामृतस्याशरीरस्यात्मनोऽधिष्ठानमात्तो वै सशरीरः
प्रियाप्रियाभ्यां न वै सशरीरस्य सतः
प्रियाप्रियाभ्यामपहतिरस्ति ॥ ८.९.३ ॥

**Transliteration**

maghavan martyan vā idaṃ śarīram āttaṃ mṛtyunā
tad asyāmṛtasyāśarīrasyātmano 'dhiṣṭhānam
ātto vai saśarīraḥ priyāpriyābhyāṃ
na vai saśarīrasya sataḥ priyāpriyābhyām apatir asti ||

**Translation**

O Maghavan, this body is indeed mortal, seized by death. Yet it is the abode of that immortal, bodiless Self. One who is embodied is subject to pleasure and pain. There is no freedom from pleasure and pain for the embodied one.

```yaml
prakriya: 'prajapati-upadesa'
pedagogical_role: 'body-limitation-pointing'
ontological_scope: 'paramarathika'
pedagogical_stage: 'manana'
cognitive_mode: 'analytic_inquiry'
adhikara_level: 'uttama'
note: "The pivot point: Prajāpati reveals why the body-based teaching is insufficient. The ātman is described as aśarīra (bodiless) and amṛta (immortal). The body is mortal (mṛtyu-seized); as long as the ātman is conflated with the body, bondage to pleasure/pain persists."
```

## Text 8.11.3

**Sanskrit**

एवं वा अरेऽयमात्मा अजरामर ब्रह्मैव संविशति
नैनं केन चन कर्मणा लिप्यते ॥ ८.११.३ ॥

**Transliteration**

evam vā are 'yam ātmā ajarāmara brahmaiva saṃviśati
nainaṃ kena cana karmaṇā lipyate ||

**Translation**

In this way, this Self — undecaying, immortal — enters into Brahman itself. No action of any kind touches it.

```yaml
prakriya: 'prajapati-upadesa'
pedagogical_role: 'atman-as-brahman'
ontological_scope: 'paramarathika'
pedagogical_stage: 'nididhyasana'
cognitive_mode: 'direct_insight'
adhikara_level: 'uttama'
```

The bodiless one is not touched by pleasure or pain. The bodiless one is indeed attained.

## Text 8.12.3

**Sanskrit**

सोऽहमत्र कृत्वा क्रतुः संस्कारात् संसारं मन्यामहे
तस्माद् य एवमेतद् विद्याद् यावन्मात्रं हि तावत् संसारं मन्यते
परं तु ब्रह्म पश्यति ॥ ८.१२.३ ॥

**Transliteration**

so 'ham atra kṛtvā kratuḥ saṃskārāt saṃsāraṃ manyāmahe
tasmād ya evam etad vidyād yāvan mātraṃ hi tāvat saṃsāraṃ manyate
paraṃ tu brahma paśyati ||

**Translation**

Acting here with will shaped by latent impressions, we take ourselves to be in transmigration. Therefore, one who knows this thus — only to that extent does he take himself to be in transmigration; beyond that he sees Brahman.

```yaml
prakriya: 'prajapati-upadesa'
pedagogical_role: 'liberation-through-knowledge'
ontological_scope: 'paramarathika'
pedagogical_stage: 'nididhyasana'
cognitive_mode: 'direct_insight'
adhikara_level: 'uttama'
note: "The final teaching: saṃsāra is a misidentification sustained by saṃskāras (latent impressions). Knowledge (vidyā) breaks this. The one who knows sees Brahman directly (paśyati) beyond the appearance of transmigration — jñāna as the sole means to liberation."
```
