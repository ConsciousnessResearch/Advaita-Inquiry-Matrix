## Text 2.1

**Sanskrit**

यदि मन्यसे सुवेदेति दभ्रमेवापि
नूनं त्वं वेत्थ ब्रह्मणो रूपम् |
यदस्य त्वं यदस्य देवेष्वथ
नु मीमांस्यमेव ते मन्ये विदितम् || १ ||

**Transliteration**

yadi manyase suvedeti dabhramevaapi
nūnaṃ tvaṃ vettha brahmaṇo rūpam |
yadasya tvaṃ yadasya deveṣvatha
nu mīmāṃsyameva te manye viditam || 1 ||

**Translation**

If you think 'I know Brahman well,' you know only a little of Its nature — you know only the form of Brahman that pertains to yourself or to the gods. Therefore Brahman must still be further inquired into by you, for I think you have not yet known It well.

## Text 2.2

**Sanskrit**

नाहं मन्ये सुवेदेति
नो न वेदेति वेद च |
यो नस्तद्वेद तद्वेद
नो न वेदेति वेद च || २ ||

**Transliteration**

nāhaṃ manye suvedeti
no na vedeti veda ca |
yo nastadveda tadveda
no na vedeti veda ca || 2 ||

**Translation**

I do not think that I know It well, nor do I think that I do not know It. He among us who knows the meaning of 'I neither know It nor do I know It' — he truly knows It.

## Text 2.3

**Sanskrit**

यस्यामतं तस्य मतं
मतं यस्य न वेद सः |
अविज्ञातं विजानतां
विज्ञातमविजानताम् || ३ ||

**Transliteration**

yasyāmataṃ tasya mataṃ
mataṃ yasya na veda saḥ |
avijñātaṃ vijānatāṃ
vijñātamavijānatām || 3 ||

**Translation**

To him by whom It is not thought out (as an object), It is known; to him by whom It is thought out (as an object), he does not know It. It is not known by those who know It; It is known by those who do not know It.

## Text 2.4

**Sanskrit**

प्रतिबोधविदितं मतम्
अमृतत्वं हि विन्दते |
आत्मना विन्दते वीर्यं
विद्यया विन्दते ऽमृतम् || ४ ||

**Transliteration**

pratibodhaviditaṃ matam
amṛtatvaṃ hi vindate |
ātmanā vindate vīryaṃ
vidyayā vindate amṛtam || 4 ||

**Translation**

It is truly known when It is known in every state of consciousness (pratibodha); for by such knowledge one attains immortality. By one's own Self one attains strength; by knowledge one attains immortality.

## Text 2.5

**Sanskrit**

इह चेदवेदीदथ सत्यमस्ति
न चेदिहावेदीन्महती विनष्टिः |
भूतेषु भूतेषु विचित्य धीराः
प्रेत्यास्माल्लोकादमृता भवन्ति || ५ ||

**Transliteration**

iha cedavedīdatha satyamasti
na cedihāvedīnmahatī vinaṣṭiḥ |
bhūteṣu bhūteṣu vicitya dhīrāḥ
pretyāsmāllokādamṛtā bhavanti || 5 ||

**Translation**

If one knows Brahman here in this life, there is truth; if one does not know It here, there is great loss. The wise, recognising Brahman in all beings, depart from this world and become immortal.
