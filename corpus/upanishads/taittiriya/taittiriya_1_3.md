## Text 1.3.1

**Sanskrit**

सह नौ यशः । सह नौ ब्रह्मवर्चसम् ।
अथातः सꣳहिताया उपनिषदम् व्याख्यास्यामः ।
पञ्चस्वधिकरणेषु ।
अधिलोकमधिज्यौतिषमधिविद्यमधिप्रजमध्यात्मम् ।
ता महासꣳहिता इत्याचक्षते । अथाधिलोकम् ।
पृथिवी पूर्वरूपम् । द्यौरुत्तररूपम् ।
आकाशः सन्धिः वायुः सन्धानम् । इत्यधिलोकम् ॥ १ ॥

**Transliteration**

saha nau yaśaḥ | saha nau brahmavarcasam |
athātaḥ saṃhitāyā upaniṣadam vyākhyāsyāmaḥ |
pañcasvadhikaraṇeṣu |
adhilokam adhijyauti ṣamadhividyam adhiprajamadhyātmam |
tā mahāsaṃhitā ityācakṣate | athādhilokam |
pṛthivī pūrvarūpam | dyauruttararūpam |
ākāśaḥ sandhiḥ | vāyuḥ sandhānam | ityadhilokam || 1 ||

**Translation**

May we both attain fame together. May spiritual pre-eminence be vouchsafed to both of us together. Now, therefore, we shall state the meditation on juxtaposition through five categories—relating  to the worlds, to the shining things, to knowledge, to progeny, and to the body. These, they call the great juxtapositions. Now then, as regards the meditation on the worlds. The earth is the first letter. Heaven is the last letter. The sky is the meeting-place.

## Text 1.3.2

**Sanskrit**

अग्निः पूर्वरूपम् । आदित्य उत्तररूपम् । आपः सन्धिः ।
वैद्युतः सन्धानम् । इत्यधिज्यौतिषम् । अथाधिविद्यम् ।
आचार्यः पूर्वरूपम् । अन्तेवास्युत्तररूपम् । विद्या सन्धिः ।
प्रवचनꣳसन्धानम् । इत्यधिविद्यम् ॥ २ ॥

**Transliteration**

agniḥ pūrvarūpam | āditya uttararūpam | āpaḥ sandhiḥ |
vaidyutaḥ sandhānam | ityadhijyautiṣam | athādhividyam |
ācāryaḥ pūrvarūpam antevāsyuttararūpam | vidyā sandhiḥ |
pravacanaṃsandhānam | ityadhividyam || 2 ||

**Translation**

Now concerning the luminaries or meditations upon fire. Fire is the prior form, the sun is the posterior form. Water is the intermediate form and lightning is the connection. That one should meditate upon light.
Now concerning knowledge: The teacher is the prior form, the taught is the posterior form, learning is the intermediate form, and the instruction is the means of joining them—thus one should meditate on learning.

## Text 1.3.3

**Sanskrit**

अथाधिप्रजम् । माता पूर्वरूपम् ।
पितोत्तररूपम् । प्रजा सन्धिः । प्रजननꣳसन्धानम् ।
इत्यधिप्रजम् ॥ ३ ॥

**Transliteration**

athādhiprajam | mātā pūrvarūpam |
pitottararūpam | prajā sandhiḥ | prajananaṃsandhānam |
ityadhiprajam || 3 ||

**Translation**

Now concerning Progeny: Mother is the prior form, father is the posterior form, progeny is the junction and procreation is the connection—thus one should meditate upon progeny.

## Text 1.3.4

**Sanskrit**

अथाध्यात्मम् । अधराहनुः पूर्वरूपम् ।
उत्तराहनूत्तररूपम् । वाक्सन्धिः । जिह्वासन्धानम् ।
इत्यध्यात्मम् । इतीमामहासꣳहिताः ।
य एवमेता महासꣳहिता व्याख्याता वे`द ।
सन्धीयते प्रजया पशुभिः ।
ब्रह्मवर्चसेनान्नाद्येन सुवर्ग्येण लोकेन ॥ ४॥
इति तृतीयोऽनुवाकः ॥

**Transliteration**

athādhyātmam | adharāhanuḥ pūrvarūpam |
uttarāhanūttararūpam | vāksandhiḥ | jihvāsandhānam |
ityadhyātmam | itīmāmahāsaṃhitāḥ |
ya evametā mahāsaṃhitā vyākhyātā ve`da |
sandhīyate prajayā paśubhiḥ |
brahmavarcasenānnādyena suvargyeṇa lokena || 4 ||
iti tṛtīyo'nuvākaḥ ||

**Translation**

What follows is concerning the individual or the body. The lower jaw is the prior form, the upper jaw the posterior form, speech is the conjunction, the tongue the means of union—thus one should meditate upon oneself. 
These are called ‘the great conjunctions’ and he who understands them as expounded here becomes united with progeny, cattle, food and the like, and with the glory of the holy lustre wealth and heavenly joys.
