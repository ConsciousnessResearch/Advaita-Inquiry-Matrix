# Aitareya Upaniṣad — Pedagogical Stage Index

## Overview

The Aitareya Upaniṣad presents a compact Advaita teaching progression that moves from cosmological origin to the direct recognition of consciousness as Brahman.

The pedagogical arc proceeds through four principal stages:

Self as the sole reality → manifestation of the cosmos → emergence of embodied experience → realization of consciousness as Brahman.

---

# Stage 1 — Absolute Self as the Origin

Location  
Chapter 1.1–1.2

Key Teaching  
The Upaniṣad opens with the declaration:

ātma vā idam eka evāgra āsīt

“In the beginning this was the Self alone.”

Pedagogical Function  
Establishes the Self as the ontological ground of existence.

# Stage 2 — Projection of the Cosmic Person

Location  
Chapter 1.3–1.4

Key Teaching  
The Self manifests the cosmic person (virāṭ) and the organs emerge together with their cosmic counterparts.

Pedagogical Function  
Demonstrates the correspondence between the macrocosm and the human body.

# Stage 3 — Embodiment and Continuity of the Self

Location  
Chapter 2.1–2.4

Key Teaching  
The Upaniṣad explains the doctrine of the three births and describes the continuity of the Self through generation and lineage.

Pedagogical Function  
Illustrates how the Self appears as individual life within the cycle of existence.

# Stage 4 — Awakening of Knowledge

Location  
Chapter 2.5–2.6

Key Teaching

Pedagogical Function

# Stage 5 — Inquiry into the Nature of the Self

Location  
Chapter 3.1

Key Teaching

# Stage 6 — Consciousness as the Basis of Mind

Location  
Chapter 3.2

Key Teaching  
Various mental functions are enumerated and declared to be expressions of consciousness.

sarvāṇy evaitāni prajñānasya nāmadheyāni

“All these are names of consciousness.”

Pedagogical Function  
Reveals consciousness as the underlying principle of cognition.
