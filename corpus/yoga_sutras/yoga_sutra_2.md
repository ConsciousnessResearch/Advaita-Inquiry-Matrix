## Text 2.1

**Sanskrit**

तपःस्वाध्यायेश्वरप्रणिधानानि क्रियायोगः ॥ २.१ ॥

**Transliteration**

tapaḥ-svādhyāya-Īśvara-praṇidhānāni kriyā-yogaḥ ||

**Translation**

Tapas, self-study, and surrender to Īśvara constitute kriyā yoga.

```yaml
prakriya: 'kriya-yoga'
pedagogical_role: 'sadhana-prescription'
ontological_scope: 'dual-register'
pedagogical_stage: 'sravana'
cognitive_mode: 'analytic_contemplative'
adhikara_level: 'madhyama'
note: "The practical triad: tapas (discipline/heat), svādhyāya (self-study/scripture study), Īśvara-praṇidhāna (surrender to the Lord). Together they constitute kriyā yoga — yoga of action, the foundation before formal meditation. Svādhyāya bridges tapas and surrender: it is both study of texts and inquiry into the self."
```

**Transliteration**

samādhi-bhāvanārthaḥ kleśa-tanūkaraṇārthaś ca ||

**Translation**

It is practiced for cultivating samādhi and for attenuating the afflictions.


## Text 2.3

**Sanskrit**

अविद्यास्मितारागद्वेषाभिनिवेशाः क्लेशाः ॥ २.३ ॥

**Transliteration**

avidyāsmitā-rāga-dveṣa-abhiniveśāḥ kleśāḥ ||

**Translation**

Avidyā, asmitā, rāga, dveṣa, and abhiniveśa are the five afflictions.

```yaml
prakriya: 'klesa-five'

ontological_scope: 'dual-register'
pedagogical_stage: 'sravana'
cognitive_mode: 'analytic_contemplative'
adhikara_level: 'madhyama'
```

## Text 2.4

**Sanskrit**

अविद्या क्षेत्रमुत्तरेषां प्रसुप्ततनुविच्छिन्नोदाराणाम् ॥ २.४ ॥

**Transliteration**

avidyā kṣetram uttareṣāṃ prasuptatanu-vicchinnodārāṇām ||

**Translation**

Avidyā is the field for the others, whether they be dormant, attenuated, intercepted, or active.

```yaml
prakriya: 'avidya-as-root'
pedagogical_role: 'root-cause-identification'
ontological_scope: 'paramarathika'
pedagogical_stage: 'manana'
cognitive_mode: 'analytic_inquiry'
adhikara_level: 'uttama'
note: "Avidyā is the kṣetra (field/soil) in which the other four kleśas grow. The four states of a kleśa — prasupta (dormant), tanu (thin), vicchinna (intercepted), udāra (active) — map a progression. Dormant kleśas are the most dangerous: they appear absent but remain as seeds (saṃskāras)."
```

## Text 2.5

**Sanskrit**

अनित्याशुचिदुःखानात्मसु नित्यशुचिसुखात्मख्यातिरविद्या ॥ २.५ ॥

**Transliteration**

anityāśuci-duḥkhānātmasu nitya-śuci-sukha-ātmakhyātir avidyā ||

**Translation**

Avidyā is taking the impermanent to be permanent, the impure to be pure, the painful to be pleasurable, and the non-self to be the Self.

```yaml
prakriya: 'avidya-definition'
pedagogical_role: 'root-ignorance-analysis'
ontological_scope: 'paramarathika'
pedagogical_stage: 'manana'
cognitive_mode: 'analytic_inquiry'
adhikara_level: 'uttama'
note: "The fourfold error of avidyā: (1) nitya/anitya confusion (permanence), (2) śuci/aśuci confusion (purity), (3) sukha/duḥkha confusion (pleasure), (4) ātman/anātman confusion (selfhood). The fourth is the root — misidentification with the non-self. The first three are its expressions."
```

**Transliteration**

dṛg-darśana-śaktyor ekātmatevāsmitā ||

**Translation**

Asmitā is as if the powers of the seer and the instrument of seeing were one.

```yaml

pedagogical_stage: 'manana'
cognitive_mode: 'analytic_inquiry'
```

## Text 2.9

**Sanskrit**

स्वरसवाही विदुषोऽपि तथारूढोऽभिनिवेशः ॥ २.९ ॥

**Transliteration**

svarasavāhī viduṣo 'pi tathārūḍho 'bhiniveśaḥ ||

**Translation**

Abhiniveśa, flowing by its own momentum, is established even in the learned.

```yaml
prakriya: 'abhinivesa-definition'
pedagogical_role: 'fear-of-death-analysis'
ontological_scope: 'dual-register'
pedagogical_stage: 'manana'
cognitive_mode: 'analytic_inquiry'
adhikara_level: 'uttama'
note: "Abhiniveśa = clinging to life, fear of annihilation. Svarasavāhī = flows by its own current (without external cause). The crucial qualification: viduṣo 'pi — even in the learned/scholars. **Transliteration**
```

**Translation**

draṣṭṛ-dṛśyayoḥ saṃyogo heya-hetuḥ ||

**Translation**

## Text 2.25

**Sanskrit**

तदभावात् संयोगाभावो हानं तद् दृशेः कैवल्यम् ॥ २.२५ ॥

**Transliteration**

tad-abhāvāt saṃyogābhāvo hānaṃ tad dṛśeḥ kaivalyam ||

**Translation**

From the absence of avidyā, there is no conjunction; that is the freedom of the seer — kaivalya.

```yaml
prakriya: 'kaivalya-through-viveka'
pedagogical_role: 'liberation-pointing'
ontological_scope: 'paramarathika'
pedagogical_stage: 'nididhyasana'
cognitive_mode: 'direct_insight'
adhikara_level: 'uttama'
note: "Kaivalya = aloneness/independence of the seer. When avidyā dissolves, saṃyoga dissolves, and the seer stands in its own nature. The causal chain: avidyā → saṃyoga → duḥkha; its reversal: viveka → no saṃyoga → kaivalya."
```

**Transliteration**

viveka-khyātir aviplavā hānopāyaḥ ||

