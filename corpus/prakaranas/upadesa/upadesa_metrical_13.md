## Text 13.1

**Sanskrit**

अचिुष्ट्व न्न दृवष्टमे तथ ऽश्रोत्रस्य ि श्रुततः ।
अव क्त्व न्न तु वक्क्तः स्य िमन्स्त्व न्मततः ि ु तः ॥१॥

**Transliteration**

acakṣuṣṭvānna dṛṣṭirme tathā'śrotrasya kā śrutiḥ |
avāktvānna tu vaktiḥ syādamanstvānmatiḥ kutaḥ ||1||

**Translation**

There is no vision in Me as I am without the organ of seeing. How can there be hearing in Me who have no auditive organ? Devoid of the organ of speech I have no action of speaking in Me. How can there be thinking in Me who have no mind?

## Text 13.2-3

**Sanskrit**

अप्र णस्य न िम यञ्स्त बुद्ध्यभ वे न वेदित । 
ववद्य ववद्ये ततो न स्तञ्श्चन्म त्रज्योततषो मम॥२॥

तनत्यमुक्तस्य शुद्धस्य ि ू टस्थस्य ववच सिनः।
अमृतस्य ििस्यैवमशिीिस्य िवयि ॥३॥

**Transliteration**

aprāṇasya na karmāsti buddhyabhāve na veditā | 
vidyāvidye tato na staścinmātrajyotiṣo mama ||2||

nityamuktasya śuddhasya kūṭasthasyāvicālinaḥ |
amṛtasyākṣarasyaivamaśarīrasya sarvadā ||3||

**Translation**

Devoid of the vital force I have no action (in Me), and devoid of the intellect I am not a knower. Ever free, ever pure, changeless, immovable, immortal, imperishable and bodiless I have no knowledge or ignorance in Me who am of the nature of the Light of Pure Consciousness only.

## Text 13.6

**Sanskrit**

## Text 13.7-8

**Sanskrit**

तथ न्येञ्न्द्रययुक्त य वृत्तयो ववशय चजन ः ।
स्मृती ि ग दिरूप च िे वि न्तमयनस्यवप ॥७॥

म नस्यस्तद्विन्यस्य दृश्यन्ते स्वप्नवृत्तयः ।
द्रुष्ट ु दृयवष्टस्ततो तनत्य शुद्ध नन्त च िे वि ॥८॥

**Transliteration**

tathānyendriyayuktā yā vṛttayo viśayāñjanāḥ |
smṛtī rāgādirūpā ca kevalāntarmanasyapi ||7||

mānasyastadvadanyasya dṛśyante svapnavṛttayaḥ |
druṣṭurdṛṣṭistato nityā śuddhānantā ca kevalā ||8||

**Translation**

Similarly, other mental modifications in the forms of objects of knowledge produced through the instrumentality of other organs and also those in the forms of memory, attachment, etc. existing only within the mind, and those again in dream are witnessed by one different from all of them (i.e., by the Self). The Knowledge, therefore, of the Knower is eternal, pure, infinite and without a second.

## Text 13.9

**Sanskrit**

अतनत्य ि ऽववशुद्धेतत गृह्यतेऽत्र वववेितः ।

**Translation**

It is through the indiscrimination between the Self and the modifications of the mind—knowable adjuncts to the Self—that the Knowledge of the Knower is wrongly conceived by the people to be impure and transitory, and the Self happy or miserable.

## Text 13.10

**Sanskrit**

## Text 13.20

**Sanskrit**

न िच्च हं न च िच्च नोभयं िे विः सशवः ।

**Translation**

I am not the three visible elements nor the two invisible ones, neither am I both (i.e., their combination, the body). I am devoid of all attributes and the three Gunas. In Me there is neither night nor day nor their juncture as I am always of the nature of light.
