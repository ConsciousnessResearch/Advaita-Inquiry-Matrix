# Upadeśa-sāhasrī Metrical Chapter 1

---

## Text 1

**Sanskrit**

चैतन्यं सर्वगं सर्ं सर्वभूतगुहाशयम ् ।
यत्सर्ववर्षयातीतं तस्मै सर्ववर्दे नमः॥१॥

**Transliteration**

caitanyaṁ sarvagaṁ sarvaṁ sarvabhūtaguhāśayam | 
yatsarvaviṣayātītaṁ tasmai sarvavide namaḥ ||1||

**Translation**

I bow down to that all-knowing One which is pure Consciousness, all-pervading, all, residing in the hearts of all beings and beyond all objects of knowledge.

------

## Text 2

**Sanskrit**

समापय्य क्रियाः सर्ाव दािाग्नन्याधानपूवर्वकाः।
ब्रह्मवर्द्यामथेदानीं र्क् ुं र्ेदः प्रचिमे॥२॥

**Transliteration**

samāpayya kriyāḥ sarvā darāgnyādhānapūrvikāḥ |
brahmavidyāmathedānīṁ vaktuṁ vedaḥ pracakrame ||2||

**Translation**

Now then the Vedas begin to describe the knowledge of Brahman after dealing with all actions preceded by marriage and the installation of sacred fire.

------

## Text 3

**Sanskrit**

कमावणि देहयोगाथं देहयोगे वप्रयावप्रये।
ध्रुर्े स्यातां ततो िागः द्वेषश्चैर् ततः क्रियाः॥३॥

**Transliteration**

karmāṇi dehayogārthaṁ dehayoge priyāpriye | 
dhruve syātāṁ tato rāgaḥ dveṣaścaiva tataḥ kriyāḥ ||3||

**Translation**

Actions, (both enjoined and prohibited), bring about one’s connection with the body; when the connection with the body has taken place pleasure and pain most surely follow.

------

## Text 4

**Sanskrit**

धमावधमौ ततोऽज्ञस्य देहयोगस्तथा पुनः ।
एर्ं ननत्यप्रर्ृतोऽयं संसािश्चिर्द्भृशम ् ॥४॥

**Transliteration**

dharmādharmau tato'jñasya dehayogastathā punaḥ |
evaṁ nityapravṛto'yaṁ saṁsāraścakravadbhṛśam ||4||

**Translation**

Thence come attraction and repulsion, from them actions follow again, as the results of which merit and demerit appertain to an ignorant man, which again are similarly followed by the connection with the body. This transmigratory existence is thus going on continually forever like a wheel.

------

## Text 5

**Sanskrit**

अज्ञानं तस्य मूलं स्याददनत तद्धानममष्यते।
ब्रह्मवर्द्यात आिब्धा ततो ननःश्रेयसं भर्ेत ् ॥५॥

**Transliteration**

ajñānaṁ tasya mūlaṁ syāditi taddhānamiṣyate |
brahmavidyāta ārabdhā tato niḥśreyasaṁ bhavet ||5||

**Translation**

The cessation of Ignorance is desirable as it is the root of this transmigratory existence. Hence a delineation of the knowledge of Brahman through which comes liberation (from Ignorance) is commenced.

------

## Text 6

**Sanskrit**

वर्द्यैर्ाज्ञानहानाय न कमावप्रनतक ू लतः।
नाज्ञानस्याप्रहािे दह िागद्वेषक्षयो भर्ेत ् ॥६॥

**Transliteration**

vidyaivājñānahānāya na karmāpratikūlataḥ |
nājñānasyāprahāṇe hi rāgadveṣakṣayo bhavet ||6||

**Translation**

Not being incompatible with Ignorance, actions do not destroy it; it is knowledge alone that does it. Ignorance not being destroyed, the destruction of desire and aversion is not possible.

------

## Text 7

**Sanskrit**

िागद्वेषक्षयाभार्े कमव दोषोद्भर्ं ध्रुर्म ् ।
तस्माग्न्नःश्रेयसाथावय वर्द्यैर्ात्र वर्धीयते॥७॥

**Transliteration**

rāgadveṣakṣayābhāve karma doṣodbhavaṁ dhruvam |
tasmānniḥśreyasārthāya vidyaivātra vidhīyate ||7||

**Translation**

Actions caused by impurities are sure to follow in case desire and aversion are not removed. Knowledge alone, therefore, is taught here so that liberation (from Ignorance) may be accomplished.

------

## Text 8

**Sanskrit**

ननु कमव तथा ननत्यं कतवव्यं जीर्ने सनत। 
वर्द्यायाः सहकारित्र्ं मोक्षं प्रनत दह तद् व्रजेत ् ॥८॥

**Transliteration**

nanu karma tathā nityaṁ kartavyaṁ jīvane sati |
vidyāyāḥ sahakāritvaṁ mokṣaṁ prati hi tad vrajet ||8||

**Translation**

(Objection) Obligatory duties should be performed (along with the practice of knowledge) as long as life lasts, because these duties cooperate with Knowledge in producing liberation.

------

## Text 9

**Sanskrit**

यथा वर्द्या तथा कमव चोददतत्र्ावर्शेषतः।
प्रत्यर्ायस्मृतेश्चैर् कायं कमव मुमुक्षुमभः॥९॥

**Transliteration**

yathā vidyā tathā karma coditatvāviśeṣataḥ |
pratyavāyasmṛteścaiva kāryaṁ karma mumukṣubhiḥ ||9||

**Translation**

As they are equally enjoined obligatory duties and knowledge (should be practised together), They should be undertaken by those who aspire after liberation because Smritis speak of sins also (arising out of the omission of those actions).

------

## Text 10

**Sanskrit**

ननु ध्रुर्फला वर्द्या नान्यग्त्कं चचदपेक्षते।
नाग्ग्ननष्टोमो यथैर्ान्यद् ध्रुर्कायोऽप्यपेक्षते ॥१०॥

**Transliteration**

nanu dhruvaphalā vidyā nānyatkiṁcidapekṣate |
nāgniṣṭomo yathaivānyad dhruvakāryo'pyapekṣate ||10||

**Translation**

(Ved*ā*ntin) But Knowledge alone gives its fixed result. 
(Opponent) No, just as the Agniṣṭomaḥ ritual gives its fixed result but also requires other (karmas)…

------

## Text 11

**Sanskrit**

तथा ध्रुर्फला वर्द्या कमव ननत्यमपेक्षते।
इत्येर्ं के चचददच्छग्न्त न कमव प्रनतक ू लतः॥११॥

**Transliteration**

tathā dhruvaphalā vidyā karma nityamapekṣate |
ityevaṁ kecidicchanti na karma pratikūlataḥ ||11||

**Translation**

…So too, Knowledge which gives its fixed result (Liberation) also expects the performance of daily duties alongside. (Reply) So some people think. (We say) No. As it is incompatible with actions, Knowledge does not depend on them (in producing its result).

------

## Text 12

**Sanskrit**

वर्द्यायाः प्रनतक ू लं दह कमव स्यात्सामभमानतः।
ननवर्वकािात्मबुवद्धश्च वर्द्येतीह प्रकीनतवता ॥१२॥

**Transliteration**

vidyāyāḥ pratikūlaṁ hi karma syātsābhimānataḥ |
nirvikārātmabuddhiśca vidyetīha prakīrtitā ||12||

**Translation**

Accompanied by egoism actions are incompatible with Knowledge. For it is well-known here (in the Vedāntas) that Knowledge is the consciousness that the Self is changeless.

------

## Text 13

**Sanskrit**

अहं कताव ममेदं स्याददनत कमव प्रर्तवते ।
र्स्त्र्धीना भर्ेदद्वद्या कत्रवधीनो भर्ेदद्वचधः ॥१३॥

**Transliteration**

ahaṁ kartā mamedaṁ syāditi karma pravartate |
vastvadhīnā bhavedvidyā kartradhīno bhavedvidhiḥ ||13||

**Translation**

Actions have their origin in the consciousness that one is a doer and has the desire of having the results of what one does. Knowledge depends on a thing, (its own object and also on evidence), while actions depend entirely on the performer.

------

## Text 14

**Sanskrit**

कािकाण्युपमृद्नानत वर्द्या बुवद्धममर्ोषिे ।
इनत तत्सत्यमादाय कमव कतुं व्यर्स्यनत ॥१४॥

**Transliteration**

kārakāṇyupamṛdnāti vidyā buddhimivoṣare |
iti tatsatyamādāya karma kartuṁ vyavasyati ||14||

**Translation**

The Knowledge (of one’s own real nature) destroys the ideas of doership etc. (on the part of oneself like the right Knowledge of the nature of the desert which destroys) the conviction of there being water in it. When this is so, how can (a man of Knowledge) accept them as true and perform actions?

------

## Text 15

**Sanskrit**

वर्रुद्धत्र्ादतः शक्यं कमव कतुं न वर्द्यया ।
सहैर् वर्दुषा तस्मात्कमव हेयं मुमुक्षुिा ॥१५॥

**Transliteration**

viruddhatvādataḥ śakyaṁ karma kartuṁ na vidyayā |
sahaiva viduṣā tasmātkarma heyaṁ mumukṣuṇā ||15||

**Translation**

It is, therefore, not possible on the part of a man of Knowledge to have Knowledge and perform an action at the same time as they are incompatible with each other. So, one who aspires after liberation should renounce actions.

------

## Text 16

**Sanskrit**

देहाद्यैिवर्शेषेि देदहनो रहिं ननजम ् ।
प्राणिनां तदवर्द्योत्थं तार्त्कमववर्चधभवर्ेत ् ॥१६॥

**Transliteration**

dehādyairaviśeṣeṇa dehino grahaṇaṁ nijam |
prāṇināṁ tadavidyotthaṁ tāvatkarmavidhirbhavet ||16||

**Translation**

The natural conviction on the part of the people that the Self is not different from the body etc. arises through Ignorance. The Vedic injunctions (and prohibitions) are authoritative as long as it prevails.

------

## Text 17

**Sanskrit**

नेनतनेतीनत देहादीनपोह्यात्मार्शेवषतः ।
अवर्शेषात्मबोधाथं तेनावर्द्या ननर्नतवता ॥१७॥

**Transliteration**

netinetīti dehādīnapohyātmāvaśeṣitaḥ |
aviśeṣātmabodhārthaṁ tenāvidyā nivartitā ||17||

**Translation**

The Self is left over by negating the body etc. by the Sruti, ‘Not this, not this,’ so that one may have the Knowledge of the Self which is devoid of all attributes. Ignorance is brought to an end by this Knowledge.

------

## Text 18

**Sanskrit**

ननर्ृत्ता सा कथं भूयः प्रसूयेत प्रमाितः ।
असत्येर्ावर्शेषेऽवप प्रत्यगात्मनन के र्ले ॥१८॥

**Transliteration**

nivṛttā sā kathaṁ bhūyaḥ prasūyeta pramāṇataḥ |
asatyevāviśeṣe'pi pratyagātmani kevale ||18||

**Translation**

How can Ignorance, once negated (by Vedic evidence), arise again? For it is neither in the innermost Self which is only one without a second and without attributes nor in the non-Self.

------

## Text 19

**Sanskrit**

न चेद्भूयः प्रसूयेत कताव भोक्े नत धीः कथम ् ।
सदस्मीनत च वर्ज्ञाने तस्मादद्वद्याऽसहानयका ॥१९॥

**Transliteration**

na cedbhūyaḥ prasūyeta kartā bhokteti dhīḥ katham |
lsadasmīti ca vijñāne tasmādvidyā'sahāyikā ||19||

**Translation**

How can there again be the idea that one is a doer of actions and experiencer of their results if Ignorance does not arise after there has grown the Knowledge, ‘I am Brahman’? Knowledge, therefore, is independent of actions (in producing liberation).

------

अत्यिेचयददत्युक्ो न्यासः श्रुत्यात एर् दह ।

## Text 21

**Sanskrit**

अमृतत्र्ं श्रुतं यस्मात्त्याज्यं कमव मुमुक्षुमभः । 
अग्ग्ननष्टोमर्ददत्युक् ं तत्रेदममभधीयते ॥२१॥

**Transliteration**

amṛtatvaṁ śrutaṁ yasmāttyājyaṁ karma mumukṣubhiḥ |

**Translation**

…supports the immortality heard of. Therefore, action should be renounced by seekers of Liberation. In response to the objection that stated similarity between Knowledge and the Agniṣṭoma ritual (verse 12), the following is explained.

## Text 22

**Sanskrit**

नैककािकसा्यत्र्ात्फलान्यत्र्ाच्च कमविः ।
वर्द्या तदद्वपिीतातो दृष्टान्तो वर्षमो भर्ेत ् ॥२२॥

**Transliteration**

naikakārakasādhyatvātphalānyatvācca karmaṇaḥ|
vidyā tadviparītāto dṛṣṭānto viṣamo bhavet ||22||

**Translation**

Knowledge is quite opposite in nature to that of actions like Agnishtoma etc. for they are accomplished with the help of many materials and differ in the quality of the result of each individual performance. The example, therefore, is not parallel.

------

## Text 23

**Sanskrit**

क ृ ष्याददर्त्फलाथवत्र्ादन्यकमोपबृंहिम ् ।
अग्ग्ननष्टोमस्त्र्पेक्षेत वर्द्यान्यग्त्कमपेक्षते ॥२३॥

**Transliteration**

kṛṣyādivatphalārthatvādanyakarmopabṛṁhaṇam |
agniṣṭomastvapekṣeta vidyānyatkimapekṣate ||23||

**Translation**

As it produces a result (variable in quality) the Agnishtoma sacrifice, like agriculture etc., requires subsidiary actions other than itself. But what else will Knowledge depend on? 

------

## Text 24

**Sanskrit**

प्रत्यर्ायस्तु तस्यैर् यस्याहंकाि इष्यते ।
अहंकािफलाचथवत्र्े वर्द्येते नात्मर्ेददनः ॥२४॥

**Transliteration**

pratyavāyastu tasyaiva yasyāhaṁkāra iṣyate |
ahaṁkāraphalārthitve vidyete nātmavedinaḥ ||24||

**Translation**

It is only one having egoism that may incur sin (by the omission of duties). A man who has got Self-knowledge has neither egoism nor a desire for the results of actions.

------

## Text 25

**Sanskrit**

तस्मादज्ञानहानाय संसािवर्ननर्ृत्तये ।
ब्रह्मवर्द्यावर्धानाय प्रािब्धोपननषग्त्त्र्यम ् ॥२५॥

**Transliteration**

tasmādajñānahānāya saṁsāravinivṛttaye |
brahmavidyāvidhānāya prārabdhopaniṣattviyam ||25||

**Translation**

The Upanishads are, therefore, commenced in order to teach the Knowledge of Brahman so that Ignorance might be removed and transmigratory existence might forever come to an end.

------

## Text 26

**Sanskrit**

सदेरुपननपूर्वस्य ग्क्र्वप चोपननषद्भर्ेत ् ।
मन्दीकििभार्ाच्च गभावदेः शातनात्तथा ॥२६॥

**Transliteration**

saderupanipūrvasya kvipi copaniṣadbhavet |
mandīkaraṇabhāvācca garbhādeḥ śātanāttathā ||26||

**Translation**

The word Upanishat is derived from the root ‘sad’ prefixed by two particles, ‘upa’ and ‘ni’ and followed by the suffix ‘kvip’. So, that which loosens the bondage of birth, (old age), etc., (enables a man to approach Brahman) and destroys birth, (death), etc., is called Upanishat.
