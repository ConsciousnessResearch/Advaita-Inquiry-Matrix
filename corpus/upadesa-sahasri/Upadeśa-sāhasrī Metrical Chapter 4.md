# Upadeśa-sāhasrī Metrical Chapter 4

---

## Text 1

**Sanskrit**

अहंप्रत्ययबीजं यदहंप्रत्ययर्ग्त्स्थतम ् । 
नाहंप्रत्ययर्ह्युष्टं कथं कमव प्रिोहनत ॥१॥

**Transliteration**

ahaṁpratyayabījaṁ yadahaṁpratyayavatśitaṁ |
nāhaṁpratyayavahnayuṣṭaṁ kathaṁ karma prarohati ||1||

**Translation**

How can those actions of which the root is egoism and which are accumulated in the mind produce results when they are burnt by the fire of non-egoism, (the right Knowledge that one is neither the doer of actions nor the experiencer of their results)?

------

## Text 2

**Sanskrit**

दृष्टर्च्चेत्प्रिोहः स्यान्नान्यकमाव स इष्यते ।
तग्न्निोधे कथं तत्स्यात्पृच्छामो र्स्तदुच्यताम ् ॥२॥

**Transliteration**

dṛṣṭavaccetprarohaḥ syānnānyakarmā sa iṣyate |
tannirodhe kathaṁ tatsyātpṛccāmo vastaducyatāṁ ||2||

**Translation**

(Objector): Actions burnt by the fire of Knowledge may produce results like the seen ones (of the actions of a man of Knowledge). 
(Reply). No. They are due to another cause.
(Objector): I ask you how there can be actions when egoism is destroyed. Please answer.

------

## Text 3

**Sanskrit**

देहाद्यािम्भसामथ्यावज्ज्ञानं सदद्वषयं त्र्नय ।
अमभभूय फलं क ु यावत्कमावन्ते ज्ञानमुद्भर्ेत ् ॥३॥

**Transliteration**

dehādyārambhasāmarthyājjñānaṁ sadviṣayaṁ tvayi |
abhibhūya phalaṁ kuryātkarmānte jñānamudbhavet ||3||

**Translation**

(Reply): Such actions produce their results by overpowering the Knowledge of Brahman in you, because they have the power of producing the body etc. Knowledge, however, becomes manifest when the results of these actions come to an end.

------

## Text 4

**Sanskrit**

आिब्धस्य फले ह्येते भोगो ज्ञानं च कमविः ।
अवर्िोधस्तयोयुवक्ो र्ैधम्यं चेतिस्य तु ॥४॥

**Transliteration**

ārabdhasya phale hyete bhogo ġyānaṁ ca karmanaḥ |
avirodhastayoryukto vaidharmyaṁ cetarasya tu ||4||

**Translation**

As knowledge and the experiencing (of pain and pleasure) are both results of actions that have given rise to the present body and have begun to produce results, it is reasonable that they are not incompatible with each other. But other kinds of actions are different in nature.

------

## Text 5

**Sanskrit**

देहात्मज्ञानर्ज्ज्ञानं देहत्मज्ञानबाधकम ् ।
आत्मन्येर् भर्ेद्यस्य स नेच्छन्नवप मुच्यते ।
ततः सर्वममदं मसद्धं प्रयोगोऽस्मामभिीरितः ॥५॥

**Transliteration**

dehātmajñānarjjñānaṃ dehatmajñānabādhakama ।
ātmanyer bharedyasya sa necchannavapa mucyate ।
tataḥ sarvamamadaṃ masaddhaṃ prayogo'smāmabhiīritaḥ ॥5॥

**Translation**

The Knowledge of one’s identity with the pure Self that negates the (wrong) notion of the identity of the body and the Self sets a man free even against his
will when it becomes as firm as the belief of the man that he is a human being. All this, therefore, is established. And reasons have been already given by us.
