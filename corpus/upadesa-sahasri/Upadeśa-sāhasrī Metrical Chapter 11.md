# Upadeśa-sāhasrī Metrical Chapter 11

---

## Text 1

**Sanskrit**

ईक्षितृत्वं स्वतःसिद्धं जन्तून ं च तत्तोऽन्यत ।
अज्ञ न दित्यतोऽन्यत्वं िििीतत तनवत्ययते ॥१॥

**Transliteration**

īkṣitṛtvaṁ svataḥsiddhaṁ jantūnāṁ ca tatto'nyatā |
ajñānādityato'nyatvaṁ sadasīti nivartyate ||1||

**Translation**

All beings are by nature pure Consciousness Itself. It is due to Ignorance that they apppear to be different from It. Their (apparent) difference from It is removed by the teaching ‘Thou art Existence.’

------

## Text 2

**Sanskrit**

एत वद्ध्यमृतत्वं न किञ्चचिन्यत्िह यिम ् ।
ज्ञ नस्येतत ब्रुवच्छ स्त्रं िसिङ्गं िमय ब धत ॥२॥

**Transliteration**

etāvaddhyamṛtatvaṁ na kiñcidanyatsahāyakam |
jñānasyeti bruvacchāstraṁ saliṅgaṁ karma bādhate ||2||

**Translation**

The scriptures negate Vedic actions with their accessories by saying ‘Knowledge alone is the cause of immortality,’ and that ‘there is nothing else to cooperate with it (in producing liberation).’

------

## Text 3-4

**Sanskrit**

िवेष ं मनिो वृत्तञ्ववशेषेण पश्यतः ।
तस्य मे तनववयि िस्य ववशेषः स्य त्िथंचन ॥३॥

मनोवृत्तं मनश्चैव स्वप्नवज्ज ग्रतीक्षितुः। 
िंप्रि िे द्वय ित्त्व ञ्च्चन्म त्रः िवयगोऽव्ययः ॥४॥

**Transliteration**

sarveṣāṁ manaso vṛttamviśeṣeṇa paśyataḥ |
tasya me nirvikārasya viśeṣaḥ syātkathaṁcana ||3||

manovṛttaṁ manaścaiva svapnavajjāgratīkṣituḥ |
saṁprasāde dvayāsattvāccinmātraḥ sarvago'vyayaḥ ||4||

**Translation**

How can there be any special property in Me who am changeless by nature and witness the modifications of the minds of all without any exception? (How can again there be any change in Me) who witness the mind and its functions in the waking state as in dream? But as there is the absence of both the mind and its functions in deep sleep I am Pure Consciousness, all-pervading and changeless.

------

## Text 5

**Sanskrit**

स्वप्नः ित्यो यथ ऽऽबोध द्देह त्मत्वं तथैव च ।
प्रत्यि िेः प्रम णत्वं ज ग्रत्स्य ि त्मवेिन त ् ॥५॥

**Transliteration**

svapnaḥ satyo yathā''bodhāddehātmatvaṁ tathaiva ca |
pratyakṣādeḥ pramāṇatvaṁ jāgratsyādātmavedanāt ||5||

**Translation**

Just as dreams appear to be true as long as one does not wake up, so, the identification of oneself with the body etc. and the authenticity of sense-perception and the like in the waking state continue as long as there is no Self-knowledge.

------

## Text 6

**Sanskrit**

व्योमवत्िवयभूतस्थो भूतिोषैववयवञ्जयतः ।
ि िी चेत ऽगुणः शुद्धो ब्रह्मैव स्मीतत िे विः ॥६॥

**Transliteration**

vyomavatsarvabhūtastho bhūtadoṣairvivarjitaḥ |
sākṣī cetā'guṇaḥ śuddho brahmaivāsmīti kevalaḥ ||6||

**Translation**

I am Brahman of the nature of pure Consciousness, without qualities, free from Ignorance, and free from the three states of waking, dream and deep sleep. Living in all beings like the ether I am the witness free from all their defects.

------

## Text 7

**Sanskrit**

नामरूपक्रियाभ्योऽन्यो तनत्यमुक्तस्वरूपव न ।
अहम त्म पिं ब्रह्म चचन्मत्रोऽहं िि द्वयः ॥७॥

**Transliteration**

nāmarūpakriyābhyo'nyo nityamuktasvarūpavān |
ahamātmā paraṁ brahma cinmatro'haṁ sadādvayaḥ ||7||

**Translation**

Ever free and different from names, forms and actions I am the supreme Brahman, the Self, consisting of pure Consciousness and always without a second.

------

## Text 8

**Sanskrit**

अहं ब्रह्म ञ्स्म ित य च भोक्त च स्मीतत ये वविुः ।
ते नष्ट ज्ञ निमयभ्य ं न ञ्स्ति ः स्युर्न िंशयः ॥८॥

**Transliteration**

ahaṁ brahmāsmi kartā ca bhoktā cāsmīti ye viduḥ |
te naṣṭā jñānakarmabhyāṁ nāstikāḥ syurna saṁśayaḥ ||8||

**Translation**

Those who think themselves to be one with Brahman and at the same time to be doers and experiencers should be regarded as fallen from both Knowledge and duties. They are, no doubt, unbelievers (in the Vedas).

------

## Text 9

**Sanskrit**

धम यधमयफिैयोग इष्टोऽदृष्टो यथ त्मनः ।
श स्त्र द्धब्रह्मत्वमप्यस्य मोिो ज्ञ न त्तथेष्यत म ् ॥९॥

**Transliteration**

dharmādharmaphalairyoga iṣṭo'dṛṣṭo yathātmanaḥ |
śāstrādbrahmatvamapyasya mokṣo jñānāttatheṣyatām ||9||

**Translation**

It must be accepted on the strength of the scriptures that the Self is Brahman, and that liberation accrues from Right Knowledge only, like the connection with the Self of the results of sin and virtue, which, though unseen is admitted (on the same authority).

------

## Text 10

**Sanskrit**

य म ह िजन द्य स्त व िन ः स्वप्निसशयसभः ।
अनुभूयन्त ् एवेह ततोऽन्यः िे विो दृसशः ॥१०॥

**Transliteration**

yā māhārajanādyāstā vāsanāḥ svapnadarśibhiḥ |
anubhūyant eveha tato'nyaḥ kevalo dṛśiḥ ||10||

**Translation**

What are called (in the Sruti) clothes coloured with turmeric etc. are nothing but mental impressions perceived by people in dream. (The Self, their illuminator, must therefore be different from them and from the subtle body in which they lie.) So the Self, pure Consciousness, (the perceiver of doership etc.,) must be different from them (in the waking state also).

------

## Text 11

**Sanskrit**

िोश दिव ववतनष्ि ृ ष्टः ि ययि िणवञ्जयतः ।
यथ सिदृयश्यते स्वप्ने तद्वद्बोद्ध स्वयंप्रभः ॥११॥

**Transliteration**

kośādiva viniṣkṛṣṭaḥ kāryakāraṇavarjitaḥ |
yathāsirdṛśyate svapne tadvadboddhā svayaṁprabhaḥ ||11||

**Translation**

Just as a sword taken out of its sheath is seen as it is, so, the Knower, the Self, is seen in dream in Its real and self-effulgent nature free from cause and effect.

------

## Text 12

**Sanskrit**

आपेष त्प्रततबुद्धस्य ज्ञस्य स्व भ वविं पिम ् ।
उक्तं नेत्य दिव क्येन िञ्ल्पतस्य पनेतृण ॥१२॥

**Transliteration**

āpeṣātpratibuddhasya jñasya svābhāvikaṁ padam |
uktaṁ netyādivākyena kalpitasyāpanetṛṇā ||12||

**Translation**

The real nature of the individual (Self) who was pushed and awakened has been described by the saying, “Not this, not this” which negates all superimposition.

------

## Text 13

**Sanskrit**

मह ि ज ियो िोि मतय यद्वत्प्रिञ्ल्पत ः ।
स्वप्ने तद्वद्धद्वयं ववद्य द्रूपं व िनय िह ॥१३॥

**Transliteration**

mahārājādayo lokā mayi yadvatprakalpitāḥ |
svapne tadvaddvayaṃ vidyādrūpaṃ vāsanayā saha ||13||

**Translation**

Just as objects of enjoyment like a great Kingship etc. are superimposed on Me in dream (and are unreal), so, the two forms (visible and invisible) with the mental impressions, are also superimposed on Me (and are similarly unreal).

------

## Text 14

**Sanskrit**

िेहसिङ्ग त्मन ि य य व िन रूवपण किय ः ।
नेततनेत्य त्मरूपत्व न्न मे ि य य किय क्वचचत ् ॥१४॥

**Transliteration**

dehaliṅgātmanā kāryā vāsanārūpiṇā kriyāḥ |
netinetyātmarūpatvānna me kāryā kriyā kvacit ||14||

**Translation**

All actions are performed by the Self which has identified Itself with the gross and the subtle bodies and which has the nature of accumulating impressions. As I am of the nature indicated by the Sruti, “Not this, not this,” actions are nowhere to be done by Me.

------

## Text 15

**Sanskrit**

न ततोऽमृतत श ञ्स्त िमयणोऽज्ञ नहेतुतः ।
मोिस्य ज्ञ नहेतुत्व न्न तिन्यिपेिते ॥१५॥

**Transliteration**

na tato'mṛtatāśāsti karmaṇo'jñānahetutaḥ |
mokṣasya jñānahetutvānna tadanyadapekṣate ||15||

**Translation**

As actions have Ignorance for their cause there is no hope from them of immortality. As liberation is caused by right Knowledge (alone) it does not depend on anything else.

------

## Text 16

**Sanskrit**

अमृतं च भयं न तं नेतीत्य त्म वप्रयो मम ।
ववपिीतमतोऽन्यद्यत्त्यजेत्तत्िकियं ततः ॥१६॥

**Transliteration**

amṛtaṃ cābhayaṃ nārtaṃ netītyātmā priyo mama |
viparītamato'nyadyattyajettatsakriyaṃ tataḥ ||16||

**Translation**

But Immortality is free from fear and destruction. The individual Self (signified by the words) ‘dear to one’ is Brahman (devoid of all attributes) according to the Sruti, ‘Not this, not this’, Whatever is thought to be different from It
should, therefore, be renounced together with all actions.
