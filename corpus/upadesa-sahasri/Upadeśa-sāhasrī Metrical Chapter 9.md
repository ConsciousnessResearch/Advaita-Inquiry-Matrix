# Upadeśa-sāhasrī Metrical Chapter 9

---

## Text 1

**Sanskrit**

सूक्ष्मताव्यावपते ज्ञेये गन्धादेरुत्तिोत्तिम ् |
प्रत्यगात्मर्सानेषु पूर्वपूर्वप्रहाितः ||१||

**Transliteration**

sūkṣmatāvyāpite jneye gandhāderuttarottaram |
pratyagātmavasāneṣu pūrva pūrvaprahāṇataḥ ||1||

**Translation**

A succeeding one in the series of earth etc. ending with the innermost Self is found to be subtler and more pervasive when a preceding one is negated.

[When we negate a preceding one we get a subtler and more pervasive one till at last the innermost Self is reached which is of the nature of Existence and Consciousness and is the material Cause of everything, and therefore, absolutely all-pervading and the subtlest.]

------

## Text 2

**Sanskrit**

शािीिा पृचथर्ी तार्द्यार्द्बाह्या प्रमाितः |
अम्ब्र्ादीनी च तत्त्र्ानन तार्ज्ज्ञेयानन क ृ त्स्नशः ||२||

**Transliteration**

śarīrā pṛthivī tāvadyāvadbāhyā pramāṇataḥ |
ambvādīnī ca tattvāni tāvajjneyāni kṛtsnaśaḥ ||2||

**Translation**

External earth is the same as that pertaining to bodies. Water etc., the other categories also are, without exception, known to be the same according to evidences.

[When all the elements either external or pertaining to bodies are ascertained to be pervaded by the Self no distinction is known to exist between the external elements and those pertaining to bodies as the Self only then exists.]

------

## Text 3

**Sanskrit**

र्य्र्ादीनां यथोत्पत्तेः पूर्ं खं-सर्वगं तथा |
अहमेकः सदा सर्वग्श्चन्मात्रः सर्वगोऽद्वयः ||३||

**Transliteration**

vayvādīnām yathotpatteḥ pūrvam kham-sarvagam tathā |
ahamekaḥ sadā sarvaścenmātraḥ sarvagoऽdvayaḥ ||3||

**Translation**

Always Pure Consciousness I am one without a second, all and all-pervading like the ether before the creation of air and other elements.

------

## Text 4

**Sanskrit**

ब्रह्माद्याः स्थार्िान्ता ये प्राणिनो मम पूः स्मृताः |
कामिोधादयो दोषा जायेिन्मे क ु तोऽन्यतः ||४||

**Transliteration**

brahmādyāḥ sthāvarāntā ye prāṇino mama pūḥ smṛtāḥ |
kāmkrodhādayo doṣā jāyeranme kutoऽnyataḥ ||4||

**Translation**

It has been ascertained that all the beings from Brahmā down to the  immovable creation are My bodies. From what other source will blemishes like
lust, anger, etc. come into Me?

------

## Text 5

**Sanskrit**

भूतदोषैः सदाऽस्पृष्टं सर्वभूतस्थमीश्र्िाम ् |
नीलं व्योम यथा बालो दुष्टं मां र्ीक्षते जनः ||५||

**Transliteration**

bhūtadoṣaiḥ sadā’spṛṣṭam sarvabhūtasthamīśvaram |
nīlam vyoma yathā bālo duṣṭam mām vīkṣte janaḥ ||5||

**Translation**

People look upon Me, the Lord residing in all beings and always untouched by their defects, as tainted (with those defects) like a boy who (erroneously) looks upon the sky as blue.

------

## Text 6

**Sanskrit**

मच्चैतन्यार्भास्यत्र्ात्सर्वप्राणिचधयां सदा |
पूमवम प्राणिनः सर्े सर्वज्ञस्य वर्पाप्मनः ||६||

**Transliteration**

maccaitanyāvabhāsyatvātsarvaprāṇidhiyām sadā |
pūrmama prāṇinaḥ sarve sarvajnasya vipāpmanaḥ ||6||

**Translation**

As the intellects of all beings are illumined by My Consciousness all beings are bodies belonging to Me who am all-knowing and free from all sins and virtues.

------

## Text 7

**Sanskrit**

जननमज्ज्ञानवर्ज्ञेयं स्र्प्नज्ञानर्ददष्यते |
ननत्यं ननवर्वषयं ज्ञानं तस्माद् द्वैतं न वर्द्यते ||७||

**Transliteration**

janimajjnānavijneyam svapnajnānavadiṣyate |
nityam nirviṣayam jnānam tasmād dvaitam na vidyate ||7||

**Translation**

Objects that come into being and are capable of being made the objects of Knowledge are as unreal as those known in dream. As duality has no (real) existence Knowledge is eternal and objectless.

------

## Text 8

**Sanskrit**

ज्ञातुज्ञावनतदहव ननत्योक्ा सुषुप्ते त्र्न्यशून्यतः |
जारज्ज्ञानतस्त्र्वर्द्यातस्तद्राह्यं चासददष्यताम ् ||८||

**Transliteration**

jnāturjnānirhi nityoktā suṣupte tvanyaśūnyataḥ |
jāgrajjnātistvavidyātastadgrāhyam cāsadiṣyatām ||8||

**Translation**

As there is nothing other than the Self in dreamless sleep it is said by the Sruti that the Consciousness of the Knower is eternal. (As Knowledge is really objectless) the knowledge of objects in the waking state must be due to Ignorance. Accept then that its objects are also unreal.

------

## Text 9

**Sanskrit**

रूपर्त्त्र्ाद्यसत्त्र्ान्न दृष्ट्यादेःकमवता यथा |
एर्ं वर्ज्ञानकमवत्र्ं भूम्नो नास्तीनत गम्यते ||९||

**Transliteration**

rūpavattvādyasattvanna dṛṣṭyādeḥ karmatā yathā |
evam vijnānkarmatvam bhūmno nāstīti gamyate ||9||

**Translation**

It is clearly understood that Brahman cannot be the object of knowledge just as It cannot be the object of seeing etc. as It has no colour, form and the like.
