# Upadeśa-sāhasrī Metrical Chapter 7

---

## Text 1

**Sanskrit**

बुद््यारूढं सदा सर्ं दृश्यते यत्र तत्र र्ा |
मया तस्मात्पिं ब्रह्म सर्वज्ञश्चाग्स्म सर्वगः ||१||

**Transliteration**

buddhyārūḍhaṁ sadā sarvaṁ dr̥śyate yatra tatra vā |
mayā tasmātparaṁ brahma sarvajñaścāsmi sarvagaḥ ||1||

**Translation**

I am the supreme Brahman all-knowing and all-pervading, as pervaded by the intellect all things in all conditions are always illumined by me.

------

## Text 2

**Sanskrit**

यथात्मबुद्चधचािािां साक्षी तद्र्त्पिेष्र्वप |
नैर्ापोढ ुं न र्ादातुं शक्यस्तस्मात्पिो ह्यहम ् ||२||

**Transliteration**

yathātmabuddhicārāṇāṁ sākṣī tadvatpareṣvapi |
naivāpoḍhuṁ na vādātuṁ śakyastasmātparo hyaham ||2||

**Translation**

Just as I am the witness of all the objects of my intellect, so, I am that of the objects of other intellects. I am not capable of being rejected or accepted. Therefore I am the supreme Brahman.

------

## Text 3

**Sanskrit**

वर्करित्र्मशुद्धत्र्ं भौनतकत्र्ं न चात्मनः |
अशेषबुद्चधसाक्षक्षत्र्ाद् बुद्चधर्च्चाल्पर्ेदना ||३||

**Transliteration**

vikaritvamaśuddhatvaṁ bhautikatvaṁ na cātmanaḥ |
aśeṣabuddhisākṣitvād buddhivaccālpavedanā ||3||

**Translation**

As It is the witness of all intellects (and their modifications) the Self is not of limited knowledge like the intellects and has no change, impurity or material nature in It.

------

## Text 4

**Sanskrit**

मिौ प्रकाश्यते यद्र्द्रक्ताद्याकाितातपे |
मनय संदृश्यते सर्वमातपेनेर् तन्मया ||४||

**Transliteration**

maṇau prakāśyate yadvadraktādyākāratātape |
mayi saṁdr̥śyate sarvamātapeneva tanmayā ||4||

**Translation**

Just as in the presence of sunlight colours such as red etc., (of flowers and other things) are manifested in a jewel, so, all objects are seen (in the intellect) in My Presence. All things are, therefore, illumined by Me like sunlight.

------

## Text 5

**Sanskrit**

बुद्धौ दृश्यं भर्ेद् बुद्धौ सत्यां नाग्स्त वर्पयवये |
द्रष्टा यस्मात्सदा द्रष्टा तस्माद् द्र्ैतं न वर्द्यते ||५||

**Transliteration**

buddhau dr̥śyaṁ bhaved buddhau satyāṁ nāsti viparyaye |
draṣṭā yasmātsadā draṣṭā tasmād dvaitaṁ na vidyate ||5||

**Translation**

Objects of knowledge exist in the intellect as long as it is there (in waking and dream); but they do not exist in the opposite case (i.e., when the intellect is merged during deep sleep). The Knower is always the knower. Duality has, therefore, no existence.

------

## Text 6

**Sanskrit**

अवर्र्ेकात्पिाभार्ं यथा बुद्चधिर्ैत्तथा |
वर्र्ेकात्तु पिादन्यः स्र्यं चावप न वर्द्यते ||६||

**Transliteration**

avivekātparābhāvaṁ yathā buddhiravaittathā |
vivekāttu parādanyaḥ svayaṁ cāpi na vidyate ||6||

**Translation**

The intellect knew the nonexistence of the supreme Brahman before the discrimination (between the Self and the non-Self). But after the discrimination there is no individual Self different from Brahman nor is there the intellect itself.
