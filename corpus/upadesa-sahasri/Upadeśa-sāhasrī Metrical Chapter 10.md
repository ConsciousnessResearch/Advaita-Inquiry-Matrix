# Upadeśa-sāhasrī Metrical Chapter 10

---

## Text 1

**Sanskrit**

दृमशस्र्रूपं गगनोपमं पिं सक ृ दद्वभातं त्र्जमेकमक्षिम ् |
अलेपकं सर्वगतं यदद्वयं तदेर् चाहं सततं वर्मुक् ॐ ||१||

**Transliteration**

dr̥śisvarūpaṁ gaganopamaṁ paraṁ sakr̥dvibhātaṁ tvajamekamakṣaram |
alepakaṁ sarvagataṁ yadadvayaṁ tadeva cāhaṁ satataṁ vimukta oṁ ||1||

**Translation**

I am the supreme Brahman which is pure consciousness, always clearly manifest, unborn, one only, imperishable, unattached and all-pervading like the ether and non-dual. I am, therefore, ever-free. Āūṁ.

------

## Text 2

**Sanskrit**

दृमशस्तु शुद्धोSहमवर्ग्क्त्रयात्मको न मेSग्स्त कग्श्चद्वर्षयः स्र्भार्तः |
पुिग्स्तिस्चो्र्वमधश्च सर्वतः संपूिवभूमा त्र्ज आत्मनन ग्स्थतः ||२||

**Transliteration**

dr̥śistu śuddho'hamaviktriyātmako na me'sti kaścidviṣayaḥ svabhāvataḥ |
purastirascordhvamadhaśca sarvataḥ saṁpūrṇabhūmā tvaja ātmani sthitaḥ ||2||

**Translation**

Pure and changeless consciousness I am by nature devoid of objects (to illumine). Unborn and established in the Self I am all-pervading Brahman in the front, oblique, upward, downward and all other directions.

------

## Text 3

**Sanskrit**

अजोSमिश्चैर् तथाSजिोSमृतः स्र्यंप्रभः सर्वगतोSहमद्वयः |
न काििं कायवमतीर् ननमवलः सदैकतृप्तश्च ततो वर्मुक् ॐ ||३||

**Transliteration**

ajo'maraścaiva tathā'jaroSmr̥taḥ svayaṁprabhaḥ sarvagato'hamadvayaḥ |
na kāraṇaṁ kāryamatīva nirmalaḥ sadaikatr̥ptaśca tato vimukta oṁ ||3||

**Translation**

I am unborn, deathless, devoid of old age, immortal, self-effulgent, all-pervading and non-dual. Perfectly pure, having neither cause nor effect and contented with the one Bliss, I am free. Āūṁ.

------

## Text 4

**Sanskrit**

सुषुप्तजारत्स्र्पतश्च दशवनं न मेऽग्स्त क्रकग्ञ्चत्स्र्ममर्ेह मोहनम ् ।
स्र्तश्च तेषां पितोऽप्यसत्त्र्ततस्तुिीय एर्ाग्स्म सदादृगद्र्यः ॥४॥

**Transliteration**

suṣuptajāgratsvapataśca darśanaṁ na me'sti kiñcitsvamiveha mohanam ।
svataśca teṣāṁ parato'pyasattvatatasturīya evāsmi sadādr̥gadvayaḥ ॥4॥

**Translation**

No perception whatever in waking, dream or deep sleep belongs to Me but it is due to delusion. For these states have no independent existence nor an existence depending’ on the Self. I am, therefore, the Fourth which is the Seer of all the three states and without a second.

------

## Text 5

**Sanskrit**

शिीिबुद्धीग्न्द्रयदुःखसंतनतनव मे न चाहं मम ननवर्वकाितः |
असत्त्र्हेतोश्च तथैर् संततेिसत्त्र्मस्याः स्र्पतो दह दृश्यर्त ् ||५||

**Transliteration**

śarīrabuddhīndriyaduḥkhasaṁtatirna me na cāhaṁ mama nirvikārataḥ |
asattvahetośca tathaiva saṁtaterasattvamasyāḥ svapato hi dr̥śyavat ||5||

**Translation**

As I am changeless the series producing pain viz., the body, the intellect and the senses are not Myself nor Mine. Moreover they are unreal like dream objects, there being a reason for inference that they are so.

------

## Text 6

**Sanskrit**

इदं तु सत्यं मम नाग्स्त वर्क्रिया वर्कािहेतुनव दह मेSद्वयत्र्तः |
न पुण्यपापे न च मोक्षबन्धने न चाग्स्त र्िावश्रमताSशिीितः ||६||

**Transliteration**

idaṁ tu satyaṁ mama nāsti vikriyā vikāraheturna hi me'dvayatvataḥ |
na puṇyapāpe na ca mokṣabandhane na cāsti varṇāśramatā'śarīrataḥ ||6||

**Translation**

But it is true that I have no change nor any cause of a change as I am without a second. As I do not possess a body I have neither sin nor virtue, neither
bondage nor liberation, neither a caste nor an order of life.

------

## Text 7

**Sanskrit**

अनाददतो ननगुवितो न कमव मे फलं च तस्मात्पिमोSहमद्वयः |
यथा नभः सर्वगतं न मलप्यते तथा ह्यहं देहगतोSवप सूक्ष्मतः||७||

**Transliteration**

an*ā*dito nirgu*ṇ*ato na karma me phala*ṁ* ca tasm*ā*tparamo'hamadvayaḥ |
yath*ā* nabha*ḥ* sarvagata*ṁ* na lipyate tath*ā* hyaha*ṁ* dehagato'pi s*ū*k*ṣ*mataḥ ||7||

**Translation**

Beginningless and devoid of attributes I have neither actions nor their results. Therefore I am the supreme One without a second. Though in a body I do not get attached on account of My subtleness like the ether which, though all-pervading, does not get tainted.

------

## Text 8

**Sanskrit**

सदा च भूतेषु समोऽहमीश्र्िः क्षिाक्षिाभ्यां पिमो ह्यथोत्तमः ।
पिात्मतत्त्र्श्च तथाद्र्योऽवप सग्न्र्पयवयेिामभमतस्त्र्वर्द्यया ॥८॥

**Transliteration**

sad*ā* ca bh*ū*te*ṣ*u samo'ham*īś*vara*ḥ* k*ṣ*ar*ā*k*ṣ*ar*ā*bhy*āṁ* paramo hyathottama*ḥ* ।
par*ā*tmatattva*ś*ca tath*ā*dvayo'pi sanviparyaye*ṇā*bhimatastvavidyay*ā* ॥ 8 ॥

**Translation**

Though I am the Lord always the same in all beings, beyond the perishable and the imperishable, and therefore the Supreme, the Self of all, and without a second I am considered to be of a contrary nature on account of Ignorance.

------

## Text 9

**Sanskrit**

अवर्द्यया भार्नया च कमवमभवर्ववर्क्त आत्माऽव्यर्चधः सुननमवलः ।
दृगाददशग्क्तप्रचचतोऽहमद्र्यः ग्स्थतः स्र्रूपे गगनं यथाचलम ् ॥९॥

**Transliteration**

avidyay*ā* bh*ā*vanay*ā* ca karmabhirvivikta *ā*tm*ā*'vyavadhi*ḥ* sunirmala*ḥ* ।
dr*̥*g*ā*di*ś*aktipracito'hamadvaya*ḥ* sthita*ḥ* svar*ū*pe gagana*ṁ* yath*ā*calam ॥ 9 ॥

**Translation**

Not distanced by anything from Itself and untouched by Ignorance, by false conceptions (of possessing a body etc.) and by actions the Self is very pure. Without a second and established in My real nature like the immovable ether I am (thought to be) connected with the powers of seeing and other perceptions.

------

## Text 10

**Sanskrit**

अहं पिं ब्रह्म वर्ननश्चयात्मदृङ् न जायते भूय इनत श्रुतेर्वचः |
न चैर् बीजे त्र्सनत प्रजायते फलं न जन्माग्स्त ततो ह्यमोहता ||१०||

**Transliteration**

aha*ṁ* para*ṁ* brahma vini*ś*cay*ā*tmadr*̥ṅ* na j*ā*yate bh*ū*ya iti *ś*rutervaca*ḥ* |
na caiva b*ī*je tvasati praj*ā*yate phala*ṁ* na janm*ā*sti tato hyamohat*ā* ||10||

**Translation**

There is the saying of the Sruti that one who has the sure conviction about oneself that one is Brahman is never born again. There being no delusion, there is no birth. For, when the cause is not there there cannot be any effect.

------

## Text 11

**Sanskrit**

ममेदममत्थं च तथेदमीदृशं तथाहमेर्ं न पिो न र्ान्यथा ।
वर्मूढतैर्ं न जनस्य कल्पना सदा समे ब्रह्मणि चाद्वये मशर्े ॥११॥

**Transliteration**

mamedamitthaṁ ca tathedamīdr̥śaṁ tathāhamevaṁ na paro na vānyathā ।
vimūḍhataivaṁ na janasya kalpanā sadā same brahmaṇi cādvaye śive ॥11॥

**Translation**

False conceptions of people such as, ‘mine,’ ‘this,’ ‘thus,’ ‘this is so,’ ‘I am so,’ ‘another is not so,’ etc., are all due to delusion. They are never in Brahman which is auspicious, the same in all and without a second.

------

## Text 12

**Sanskrit**

यदद्वयं ज्ञानमतीर् ननमवलं महात्मनां तत्र न शोकमोहता ।
तयोिभार्े न दह कमव जन्म र्ा भर्ेदयं र्ेदवर्दां वर्ननश्चयः ॥१२॥

**Transliteration**

yadadvayaṁ jñānamatīva nirmalaṁ mahātmanāṁ tatra na śokamohatā ।
tayorabhāve na hi karma janma vā bhavedayaṁ vedavidāṁ viniścayaḥ ॥12॥

**Translation**

All grief and delusion are removed from those great souls when there arises the very pure knowledge of the non-dual Self. It is the conclusion of those who know the meaning of the Vedas that there cannot be any action or birth in the absence of grief and delusion.

------

## Text 13

**Sanskrit**

सुषुप्तर्ज्जारनत यो न पश्यनत द्वयं तु पश्यन्नवप चाद्वयत्र्तः ।
तथा च क ु र्वन्नवप ननग्ष्ियश्च यः स आत्मवर्न्नान्य इतीह ननश्चयः ॥१३॥

**Transliteration**

suṣuptavajjāgrati yo na paśyati dvayaṁ tu paśyannapi cādvayatvataḥ ।
tathā ca kurvannapi niṣkriyaśca yaḥ sa ātmavinnānya itīha niścayaḥ ॥13॥

**Translation**

It is the conclusion here (in the Vedānta) that one who, though perceiving the world of duality in the waking state, does not, as a man in deep sleep does not, perceive it owing to duality being negated and who is (really) actionless even when (apparently) acting, is a man of Self-knowledge; but no one else is so.

------

## Text 14

**Sanskrit**

इतीदमुक् ं पिमाथवदशवनं मया दह र्ेदान्तवर्ननग्श्चतं पिम ् ।
वर्मुच्यतेऽग्स्मन्यदद ननग्श्चतो भर्ेन्न मलप्यते व्योम इर्ेह कमवमभः ॥१४॥

**Transliteration**

itīdamuktaṁ paramārthadarśanaṁ mayā hi vedāntaviniścitaṁ param ।
vimucyate'sminyadi niścito bhavenna lipyate vyoma iveha karmabhiḥ ॥14॥

**Translation**

This Right knowledge described by me is the highest because it is ascertained in the Vedānta. One becomes liberated and unattached (to actions) like the ether if one is perfectly convinced of this Truth.
