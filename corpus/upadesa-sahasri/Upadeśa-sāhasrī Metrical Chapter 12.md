# Upadeśa-sāhasrī Metrical Chapter 12

---

## Text 1

**Sanskrit**

प्रि शस्थं यथ िेहं ि िोिमसभमन्यते ।
द्रष्ट भ िं तथ चचत्तं द्रष्ट हसमतत मन्यते॥१॥

**Transliteration**

prakāśasthaṁ yathā dehaṁ sālokamabhimanyate |
draṣṭābhāsaṁ tathā cittaṁ draṣṭāhamiti manyate ||1||

**Translation**

Just as a man (erroneously) looks upon his body placed in the sun as having the property of light in it, so, he looks upon the intellect pervaded by the reflection of Pure Consciousness as the Self, the witness.

------

## Text 2

**Sanskrit**

यिेव दृश्यते िोिे तेन सभन्नत्वम त्मनः । 
प्रपद्यते ततो मूढस्तेन त्म नं न ववन्ितत ॥२॥

**Transliteration**

yadeva dṛśyate loke tenābhinnatvamātmanaḥ |
prapadyate tato mūḍhastenātmānaṁ na vindati ||2||

**Translation**

The Self gets identified with whatever is seen in the world. It is for this reason that an ignorant man does not know himself (to be Brahman).Brahman).

[The reason why people mistake the combination of the subtle and the gross bodies for the Self is this identification caused by Its reflection. On account of there being the reflection of Pure Consciousness in the body, the senses, the mind, the intellect and the vital force, they appear to be conscious and cannot, therefore, be discriminated from the Self.]

------

## Text 3

**Sanskrit**

िशमस्य नव त्मत्वप्रततपवत्तवि त्मनः । 
दृश्येषु तद्विेव यं मूढो िोिो न च न्यथ ॥३॥

**Transliteration**

daśamasya navātmatvapratipattivadātmanaḥ |
dṛśyeṣu tadvadevāyaṁ mūḍho loko na cānyathā ||3||

**Translation**

An ignorant man gets identified with objects of knowledge and does not know the Self (which is different from them) like the tenth boy who got identified as it were with the other nine.

------

## Text 4

**Sanskrit**

त्वं ि ु रु त्वं तिेवेतत प्रत्यय वेिि सििौ ।
एिनीडौ िथं स्य त ं ववरुद्धौ न्य यतो वि ॥४॥

**Transliteration**

tvaṁ kuru tvaṁ tadeveti pratyayāvekakālikau |
ekanīḍau kathaṁ syātāṁ viruddhau nyāyato vāda ||4||

**Translation**

Say how there can reasonably by the two contrary ideas, ‘You do this’ and ‘You are Brahman’ at the same time and in respect of the same person.

------

## Text 5

**Sanskrit**

िेह सभम तननो िुःखं न िेहस्य स्वभ वतः ।
स्व पवत्तत्प्रह ण य तत्त्वसमत्युच््ते दृशेः ॥५॥

**Transliteration**

dehābhimānino duḥkhaṁ nādehasya svabhāvataḥ |
svāpavattatprahāṇāya tattvamityucyte dṛśeḥ ||5||

**Translation**

Pain belongs to one identifying oneself with the body. One not identifying oneself with it, as in deep sleep, is, therefore, by nature free from pain. The teaching, ‘Thou art That’ is imparted in order that this identification might be removed from the Self.

------

## Text 6

**Sanskrit**

दृशेश्छ य यि रूढ मुखच्छ येव िशयने ।
पश्यंस्तं प्रत्ययं योगी दृष्ट आत्मेतत मन्यते ॥६॥

**Transliteration**

dṛśeśchāyā yadārūḍhā mukhacchāyeva darśane |
paśyaṁstaṁ pratyayaṁ yogī dṛṣṭa ātmeti manyate ||6||

**Translation**

An ignorant person looks upon the intellect as the Self, when there is the reflection of the Self in the intellect like that of a face in a mirror.

------

## Text 7

**Sanskrit**

तं च मूढं च यद्यन्यं प्रत्ययं वेवत्त नो दृशेः ।
ि एव योचगन ं प्रेष्ठो नेतिः स्य न्न िंशयः ॥७॥

**Transliteration**

taṁ ca mūḍhaṁ ca yadyanyaṁ pratyayaṁ vetti no dṛśeḥ |
sa eva yogināṁ preṣṭho netaraḥ syanna saṁśayaḥ ||7||

**Translation**

He who looks upon the ego, the indiscrimination that produces delusion and other mental modifications (or the reflection of the Self in them) as having no connection with the Self, is, without doubt, the dearest to the knowers of Brahman. No one else is so.

------

## Text 8

**Sanskrit**

ववज्ञ तेययस्तु ववज्ञ त ि त्वसमत्युच्यते यतः ।
ि स्य िनुभवस्तस्य ततोऽन्योऽनुभवो मृष ॥८॥

**Transliteration**

vijñāteryastu vijñātā sa tvamityucyate yataḥ |
sa syādanubhavastasya tato'nyo'nubhavo mṛṣā ||8||

**Translation**

As it is the Knower of knowledge that is referred to by the word ‘Thou’ (in the Sruti) the understanding of the term ‘Thou’ in this sense is correct. The other sense different from it is due to superimposition.

------

## Text 9

**Sanskrit**

दृसशरूपे िि तनत्ये िशयन िशयने मतय ।
िथं स्य त ं ततो न न्य इष्यतेऽनुभवस्ततः ॥९॥

**Transliteration**

dṛśirūpe sadānitye darśanādarśane mayi |
kathaṁ syātāṁ tato nānya iṣyate'nubhavastataḥ ||9||

**Translation**

How can there be knowledge or ignorance in Me who am eternal and always of the nature of Pure Consciousness? No knowledge, therefore, other than the Self can be accepted.

------

## Text 10

**Sanskrit**

यत्स्थस्त पो िवेिेहे दृशेः ि ववषयो यथ ।
ित्त्वस्थस्तद्विेवेह दृशेः ि ववषयस्तथ ॥१०॥

**Transliteration**

yatsthastāpo raverdehe dṛśeḥ sa viṣayo yathā |
sattvasthastadvadeveha dṛśeḥ sa viṣayastathā ||10||

**Translation**

Just as the heat of the sun (in a part of the body) together with that part of the body is the object of the knower, so, pain and pleasure together with the intellect in which they lie are the objects of the Self.

------

## Text 11

**Sanskrit**

प्रततवषद्धे दृगंशोऽज्ञः खसमवैिििोऽद्वयः ।
तनत्यमुक्तः िि शुद्धः िोऽहं ब्रह्म ञ्स्म िे विः ॥११॥

**Transliteration**

pratiṣiddhe dṛgaṁśo'jñaḥ khamivaikaraso'dvayaḥ |
nityamuktaḥ sadā śuddhaḥ so'haṁ brahmāsmi kevalaḥ ||11||

**Translation**

I am Brahman without attributes, ever pure, ever free, non-dual, homogeneous like the ether and of the nature of Consciousness from which the object portion has been negated.

------

## Text 12

**Sanskrit**

ववज्ञ तुनैव ववज्ञ त पिोऽन्यः िंभवत्यतः ।
ववज्ञ त हं पिो मुक्तः िवयभूतेषु िवयि ॥१२॥

**Transliteration**

vijñāturnaiva vijñātā paro'nyaḥ saṁbhavatyataḥ |
vijñātāhaṁ paro muktaḥ sarvabhūteṣu sarvadā ||12||

**Translation**

I am always the free supreme Knower in all beings inasmuch as there cannot be a more comprehensive knower different from Me.

------

## Text 13

**Sanskrit**

यो वेि िुप्तदृवष्टत्वम त्म नोऽितृयत ं तथ ।
ब्रह्मववत्त्वं तथ मुक्त्व ि आत्म ज्ञो न चेतिः ॥१३॥

**Transliteration**

yo vedāluptadṛṣṭitvamātmāno'kartṛtāṁ tathā |
brahmavittvaṁ tathā muktvā sa ātmājño na cetaraḥ ||13||

**Translation**

He who Knows that the Consciousness of the Self never ceases to exist, and that It is never an agent and also gives up the egoism that he is a Knower of Brahman is a (real) knower of the Self. Others are not so.

------

## Text 14

**Sanskrit**

ज्ञ तैव हमववज्ञेयः शुद्धो मुक्तः ििेत्यवप ।
वववेिी प्रत्ययो बुद्धेदृयश्यत्व न्न शव न्यतः ॥१४॥

**Transliteration**

jñataivāhamavijñeyaḥ śuddho muktaḥ sadetyapi |
vivekī pratyayo buddherdṛśyatvānnāśavānyataḥ ||14||

**Translation**

Capable by no means of being known I am the knower and always free and pure as the discriminating knowledge is in the intellect and liable to be destroyed on account of its being an object of Knowledge.

------

## Text 15

**Sanskrit**

अिुप्त त्व त्मनो दृवष्टनोत्प द्य ि ििै ययतः ।
दृश्यय च न्यय दृष्ट्य जन्यत स्य ः प्रिञ्ल्पत ॥१५॥

**Transliteration**

aluptā tvātmano dṛṣṭirnotpādyā kārakairyataḥ |
dṛśyayā cānyayā dṛṣṭyā janyatāsyāḥ prakalpitā ||15||

**Translation**

The Consciousness of the Self, on the other hand, never goes out of existence and is not capable of being produced by the action of agents etc. inasmuch as producibility is superimposed on It by another consciousness, Its object and different from It.

------

## Text 16

**Sanskrit**

िेह त्मबुद्ध्यपेित्व ि त्मनः ितृयत मृष ।
नैव किञ्चचत्ििोमीतत ित्य बुवद्धः प्रम णज ॥१६॥

**Transliteration**

dehātmabuddhyapekṣatvādātmanaḥ kartṛtā mṛṣā |
naiva kiñcitkaromiti satyā buddhiḥ pramāṇajā ||16||

**Translation**

The doership of the Self is false as it depends on the misconception of the body being the Self. That I do not do anything is the true knowledge which arises from the right evidence (the Vedas).

------

## Text 17

**Sanskrit**

ितृयत्वं ि िि पेिमितृयत्वं स्वभ वतः ।
ित य भोक्ते तत ववज्ञ नं मृषैवेतत िुतनञ्श्चतम ् ॥१७॥

**Transliteration**

kartṛtvaṁ kārakāpekṣamakartṛtvaṁ svabhāvataḥ |
kartā bhokteti vijñānaṁ mṛṣaiveti suniścitam ||17||

**Translation**

Agency depends on doership, instruments etc. but non-agency is natural. It has, therefore, been very well ascertained that the knowledge that one is a doer and experiencer is certainly false.

------

## Text 18

**Sanskrit**

एवं श स्त्र नुम न भ्य ं स्वरूपेऽवगते ितत ।
तनयोज्योऽहसमतत ह्येष ित्य बुवद्धः िथं भवेत ॥१८॥

**Transliteration**

evaṁ śāstrānumānābhyāṁ svarūpe'vagate sati |
niyojyo'hamiti hyeṣā satyā buddhiḥ kathaṁ bhavet ||18||

**Translation**

How can the idea that I am a person to be enjoined (by the Vedas to perform actions) be true when the real nature of the Self is thus known from the scriptures and inference?

------

## Text 19

**Sanskrit**

यथ िव यन्तिं व्योम व्योवनोऽप्यभ्यन्तिो ह्यहम ।
तनववयि िोऽचिः शुद्धोऽजिो मुक्तः िि द्वयः॥१९॥

**Transliteration**

yathā sarvāntaraṁ vyoma vyomno'pyabhantaro hyaham |
nirvikāro'calaḥ śuddho'jaro muktaḥ sadādvayaḥ ||19||

**Translation**

Just as the ether is in the interior of all, so, I am in the interior even of the ether. Therefore I am without change, without motion, pure, devoid of old age, ever free and without a second.
