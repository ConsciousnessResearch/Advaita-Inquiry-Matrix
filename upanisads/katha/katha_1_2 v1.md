## Mantra 1


**Sanskrit**

श्रेयश्च प्रेयश्च मनुष्यमेतः तौ सम्परीत्य विविनक्ति धीरः । श्रेयः हि
धीरः अभि प्रेयसो वृणीते प्रेयो मन्दो योगक्षेमात् वृणीते ॥ १ ॥

**Transliteration**

śreyaś ca preyaś ca manuṣyam etas tau samparītya vivinakti dhīraḥ |
śreyaḥ hi dhīraḥ abhi preyaso vṛṇīte preyo mando yogakṣemāt vṛṇīte ||
1 ||

**Translation**

Both the good (śreyaḥ) and the pleasant (preyaḥ) approach a person. The
wise, having examined them, discriminates between the two. The wise
chooses the good over the pleasant; the dull chooses the pleasant for
the sake of body and sense preservation.

## Mantra 2


**Sanskrit**

स त्वं प्रियां प्रियारूपांश्च कामान् अभिध्यायन् नचिकेतोऽत्यस्राक्षीः ।
नैतां सृङ्कां वित्तमयीमवाप्तो यस्यामज्जन्ति बहवो मनुष्याः ॥ २ ॥

**Transliteration**

sa tvaṃ priyān priyarūpāṃś ca kāmān abhidhyāyan naciketo 'tyasrākṣīḥ |
naitāṃ sṛṅkāṃ vittamayīm avāpto yasyāṃ majjanti bahavo manuṣyāḥ || 2
||

**Translation**

O Nachiketas, you have renounced the desirable and pleasant objects, and
the chain of wealth, in which many men sink.

## Mantra 3


**Sanskrit**

दूरमेते विपरीते विषूची अविद्या या च विद्येति ज्ञाता । विद्याभीप्सिनं
नचिकेतसं मन्ये न त्वा कामा बहवोऽलोलुपन्त ॥ ३ ॥

**Transliteration**

naciketaṃ manye na tvā kāmā bahavo 'lolupanta || 3 ||

**Translation**

Widely divergent and leading to different ends are what are known as
ignorance and knowledge. I consider Nachiketas to be one who seeks
knowledge; many desires have not tempted you.

## Mantra 5


**Sanskrit**

न साम्परायः प्रतिभाति बालं प्रमाद्यन्तं वित्तमोहेन मूढम् । अयं लोको

**Transliteration**

**Translation**

## Mantra 6

## Mantra 7


**Sanskrit**

न नरेणावरेण प्रोक्त एष सुविज्ञेयो बहुधा चिन्त्यमानः । अनन्यप्रोक्ते
गतिरत्र नास्ति अणीयान् ह्यतर्क्यमणुप्रमाणात् ॥ ७ ॥

**Transliteration**

na nareṇāvareṇa prokta eṣa suvijñeyo bahudhā cintyamānaḥ | ananyaprokte
gatir atra nāsti aṇīyān hy atarkyam aṇupramāṇāt || 7 ||

**Translation**

When taught by an inferior person, this Self is not well understood,
since It is thought of in many ways. When taught by one who has realized
It, there is no further doubt. It is subtler than the subtle and beyond
reasoning.

## Mantra 22

aśarīram̐ śarīreṣvanavastheṣvavasthitaṃ | 
mahāntaṃ vibhumātmānaṃ matvā dhīro na śocati || 22 ||

## Mantra 23


नायमात्मा प्रवचनेन लभ्यो न मेधया न बहुना श्रुतेन । 
यमेवैष वृणुते तेन लभ्यस्तस्यैष आत्मा विवृणुते तनूँ स्वाम् ॥ २३ ॥

nāyamātmā pravacanena labhyo na medhayā na bahunā śrutena | 
yamevaiṣa vṛṇute tena labhyastasyaiṣa ātmā vivṛṇute tanūm̐ svām || 23 ||

## Mantra 24


नाविरतो दुश्चरितान्नाशान्तो नासमाहितः । 
नाशान्तमानसो वापि प्रज्ञानेनैनमाप्नुयात् ॥ २४ ॥

nāvirato duścaritānnāśānto nāsamāhitaḥ | 
nāśāntamānaso vāpi prajñānenainamāpnuyāt || 24 ||

24. None who has not turned away from bad conducts whose senses are not under control, whose mind is not collected, or whose mind is not at rest, can attain this *atman* by knowledge.

## Mantra 25


यस्य ब्रह्म च क्शत्रं च उभे भवत ओदनः । 
मृत्युर्यस्योपसेचनं क इत्था वेद यत्र सः ॥ २५ ॥

yasya brahma ca kśatraṃ ca ubhe bhavata odanaḥ | 
mṛtyuryasyopasecanaṃ ka itthā veda yatra saḥ || 25 ||

25. Of whom, the *Brahma* and the *Kshatriya* classes are the food, and Death but pickles (to supplement it); how can one thus know where that *atman* is.
