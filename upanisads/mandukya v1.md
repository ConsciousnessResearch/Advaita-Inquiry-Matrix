## Mantra 1

<!--
prakriyā: ontological-compression
pedagogical_role: totality-declaration
ontological_scope: dual-register
relational_mode: category-transcendent
-->
**Sanskrit**

हरिः ओम् । ओमित्येतदक्षरमिदं सर्वं तस्योपव्याख्यानं भूतं भवद्भविष्यदिति सर्वमोङ्कार एव | यच्चान्यत्त्रिकालातीतं तदप्योङ्कार एव ॥ १ ॥

**Transliteration**

hariḥ oṃ | om ity etad akṣaram idaṃ sarvaṃ tasyopavyākhyānaṃ bhūtaṃ bhavadbhaviṣyaditi sarvamoṅkāra eva  | yaccānyattrikālātītaṃ tadapyoṅkāra eva || 1 ||

**Translation**

Hariḥ Aum. *Aum*, the word, is all this. A clear explanation of it (is the following). All that is past, present and future is verily *Aum*. That which is beyond the triple conception of time, is also truly *Aum*.

## Mantra 2

<!--
prakriyā: identity
pedagogical_role: mahāvākya-compression
ontological_scope: pāramārthika
relational_mode: advaita-explicit
-->
**Sanskrit**

सर्वं ह्येतद् ब्रह्मायमात्मा ब्रह्म सोऽयमात्मा चतुष्पात् ॥ २ ॥

**Transliteration**

*sarvaṃ hyetad brahmāyam ātmā brahma so 'yamātmā catuṣpāt* || 2 ||

**Translation**

All this is verily Brahman. This *ātman* is Brahman. This *ātman* has four quarters.

---

## Mantra 3

<!--
prakriyā: avasthā-analysis
pedagogical_role: experiential-mapping
ontological_scope: vyavahāra
relational_mode: performative-transition
-->
**Sanskrit**

जागरितस्थानो बहिष्प्रज्ञः सप्ताङ्ग एकोनविंशतिमुखः स्थूलभुग्वैश्वानरः प्रथमः पादः ॥ ३ ॥

**Transliteration**

jāgarita-sthānaḥ bahiḥ-prajñaḥ saptāṅga ekonaviṃśatimukhaḥ sthūlabhugvaiśvānaraḥ prathamaḥ pādaḥ || 3 ||

**Translation**

The first quarter (pāda) is Vaiśvānara, whose sphere is the waking state, who is conscious of external objects, who has seven limbs and nineteen mouths, and whose experience consists of gross objects.

## Mantra 4

<!--
prakriyā: avasthā-analysis

ontological_scope: vyavahāra

-->
**Sanskrit**

स्वप्नस्थानोऽन्तः प्रज्ञाः सप्ताङ्ग एकोनविंशतिमुखः प्रविविक्तभुक्तैजसो द्वितीयः पादः ॥ ४ ॥

**Transliteration**

svapna-sthānaḥ antaḥ-prajñaḥ saptāṅga ekonaviṃśati-mukhaḥ praviviktabhuk taijasaḥ dvitīyaḥ pādaḥ || 4 ||

**Translation**

The second quarter (pāda) is Taijasa, whose sphere is the dream state, who is conscious of internal objects, who has seven limbs and nineteen mouths, and who experiences subtle objects.

## Mantra 5

<!--
prakriyā: avasthā-analysis
pedagogical_role: causal-layer-identification
ontological_scope: vyavahāra
relational_mode: performative-transition
-->
**Sanskrit**

यत्र सुप्तो न कञ्चन कामं कामयते न कञ्चन स्वप्नं पश्यति तत्सुषुप्तम् । सुषुप्तस्थान एकीभूतः प्रज्ञानघन एवानन्दमयो ह्यानन्दभुक् चेतोमुखः प्राज्ञस्तृतीयः पादः ॥ ५ ॥

**Transliteration**

yatra suptaḥ na kañcana kāmaṁ kāmayate na kañcana svapnaṁ paśyati tat suṣuptam |
suṣupta-sthānaḥ ekībhūtaḥ prajñāna-ghana eva ānanda-mayaḥ hi ānanda-bhuk ceto-mukhaḥ prājñaḥ tṛtīyaḥ pādaḥ || 5 ||

**Translation**

That is the state of deep sleep, wherein the sleeper does not desire any objects nor see any dream. The third quarter (pāda) is Prājña, whose sphere is deep sleep, in whom all experiences become undifferentiated, who is a mass of consciousness, who is full of bliss and who experiences bliss, and who is the gateway to the knowledge of the other states.

## Mantra 6

<!--
prakriyā: scope-clarification
pedagogical_role: causal-integration
ontological_scope: scope-clarification
relational_mode: performative-transition
-->
**Sanskrit**

एष सर्वेश्वरः एष सर्वज्ञ एषोऽन्तर्याम्येष योनिः सर्वस्य प्रभवाप्ययौ हि भूतानाम् ॥ ६ ॥

**Transliteration**

eṣa sarveśvaraḥ eṣa sarvajñaḥ eṣaḥ antaryāmī eṣa yoniḥ sarvasya prabhava-apyayau hi bhūtānām || 6 ||

**Translation**

This is the Lord of all; this is the knower of all; this is the inner controller; this is the source of all; and this is that from which all beings arise and into which they dissolve.

## Mantra 7

<!--
prakriyā: apavāda
pedagogical_role: radical-negation
ontological_scope: pāramārthika
relational_mode: non-differentiable
-->
**Sanskrit**

नान्तःप्रज्ञं न बहिःप्रज्ञं नोभयतःप्रज्ञं न प्रज्ञानघनं न प्रज्ञं नाप्रज्ञम् । अदृश्यमव्यवहार्यमग्राह्यमलक्षणमचिन्त्यमव्यपदेश्यमेकात्मप्रत्ययसारं प्रपञ्चोपशमं शान्तं शिवमद्वैतं चतुर्थं मन्यन्ते स आत्मा स विज्ञेयः ॥ ७ ॥

**Transliteration**

na antaḥ-prajñam na bahiḥ-prajñam na ubhayataḥ-prajñam na prajñāna-ghanam na prajñam na aprajñam |
adṛśyam avyavahāryam agrāhyam alakṣaṇam acintyam avyapadeśyam eka-ātma-pratyaya-sāram prapañca-upaśamam śāntam śivam advaitam caturtham manyante sa ātma sa vijñeyaḥ || 7 ||

**Translation**

Turīya is not that which is conscious of the internal world, nor that which is conscious of the external world, nor that which is conscious of both, nor that which is a mass of consciousness, nor that which is simple consciousness, nor that which is insentient. It is unseen, beyond empirical dealings, incomprehensible, not graspable, without characteristics, unthinkable, indescribable, essentially of the nature of the Self alone, the cessation of all phenomena, peaceful, auspicious, and non-dual. This is known as the fourth (turīya). This is the ātman and it is to be realized.

## Mantra 8

<!--
prakriyā: symbolic-reassembly
pedagogical_role: structural-mirroring
ontological_scope: dual-register
relational_mode: performative-transition
-->
**Sanskrit**

सोऽयमात्माध्यक्षरमोङ्कारोऽधिमात्रं पादा मात्रा मात्राश्च पादा अकार उकारो मकार इति ॥ ८ ॥

**Transliteration**

saḥ ayam ātmā adhyakṣaram oṅkāraḥ adhīmātram pādā mātrā mātrāḥ ca pādāḥ akāra ukāraḥ makāra iti || 8 ||

**Translation**

This very ātman is also Oṁ from the standpoint of syllables. The quarters are the letters, and the letters are the quarters. The letters are A, U, and M.

## Mantra 9

<!--
prakriyā: symbolic-correlation
pedagogical_role: contemplative-assimilation
ontological_scope: vyavahāra
relational_mode: performative-transition
-->
**Sanskrit**

जागरितस्थानो वैश्वानरोऽकारः प्रथमा मात्राऽऽप्तेरादिमत्त्वाद्वाप्नोति ह वै सर्वान्कामानादिश्च भवति य एवं वेद ॥ ९ ॥

**Transliteration**

jāgarita-sthānaḥ vaiśvānaraḥ akāraḥ prathamā mātrā āpteḥ ādimattvāt vā āpnoti ha vai sarvān kāmān ādiś ca bhavati yaḥ evaṁ veda || 9 ||

**Translation**

Vaiśvānara, whose sphere is the waking state, is A, the first letter of Oṁ, because of pervasiveness and primacy. One who knows this attains all desires and becomes foremost.

## Mantra 10

<!--
prakriyā: symbolic-correlation
pedagogical_role: contemplative-assimilation
ontological_scope: vyavahāra
relational_mode: performative-transition
-->
**Sanskrit**

स्वप्नस्थानस्तैजस उकारो द्वितीया मात्रोत्कर्षादुभयत्वाद्वोत्कर्षति ह वै ज्ञानसन्ततिं समानश्च भवति नास्याब्रह्मवित्कुले भवति य एवं वेद ॥ १० ॥

**Transliteration**

svapna-sthānaḥ taijasaḥ ukāraḥ dvitīyā mātrā utkarṣāt ubhayatvāt vā utkarṣati ha vai jñāna-santatim samānaḥ ca bhavati na asya abrahmavit kule bhavati yaḥ evaṁ veda || 10 ||

**Translation**

Taijasa, whose sphere is the dream state, is U, the second letter of Oṁ, because of superiority and intermediate position. One who knows this attains a continuity of knowledge, becomes equal to all, and no one in his lineage remains ignorant of Brahman.

## Mantra 11

<!--
prakriyā: symbolic-correlation
pedagogical_role: contemplative-assimilation
ontological_scope: vyavahāra
relational_mode: performative-transition
-->
**Sanskrit**

सुषुप्तस्थानः प्राज्ञो मकारस्तृतीया मात्रा मितेरपीतेर्वा मिनोति ह वा इदं सर्वमपीतिश्च भवति य एवं वेद ॥ ११ ॥

**Transliteration**

suṣupta-sthānaḥ prājñaḥ makāraḥ tṛtīyā mātrā miteḥ apīteḥ vā minoti ha vā idaṁ sarvam apītiḥ ca bhavati yaḥ evaṁ veda || 11 ||

**Translation**

Prājña, whose sphere is deep sleep, is M, the third letter of Oṁ, because it is the measure and that in which all dissolves. One who knows this measures all and comprehends all within oneself.

## Mantra 12

<!--
prakriyā: symbolic-collapse
pedagogical_role: silence-consummation
ontological_scope: pāramārthika
relational_mode: non-differentiable
-->
**Sanskrit**

अमात्रश्चतुर्थोऽव्यवहार्यः प्रपञ्चोपशमः शिवोऽद्वैत एवमोङ्कार आत्मैव संविशत्यात्मनाऽऽत्मानं य एवं वेद ॥ १२ ॥

**Transliteration**

amātraḥ caturthaḥ avyavahāryaḥ prapañca-upaśamaḥ śivaḥ advaitaḥ evam oṅkāraḥ ātma eva saṁviśati ātmanā ātmānam yaḥ evaṁ veda || 12 ||

**Translation**

That which is without parts, beyond empirical dealings, the cessation of all phenomena, peaceful, auspicious, and non-dual—this is Oṁ and this is the ātman. One who knows this merges the self into the Self.
